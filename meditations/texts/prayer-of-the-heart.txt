Find a position where the body can be both relaxed and alert.
Let the spine be naturally upright, the shoulders soft, the hands resting open.
Close your eyes gently, as if lowering a curtain on the outer world.
Take a slow breath and let the body settle into this moment of quiet.

You are entering a time of deep inner stillness and receptivity.
There is nothing to achieve here, no problem to solve, no performance required.
You are simply making yourself available to a presence greater than your own.
Let that intention rest softly in your heart like a seed in warm soil.

Now choose a sacred word, a word that represents your consent to this presence.
It might be a word like peace, love, stillness, trust, grace, or surrender.
Choose whatever word rises naturally, without overthinking it.
This word is not a mantra to repeat; it is a gentle symbol of your intention.

Let the word settle into the center of your chest, in the heartspace.
It rests there lightly, like a leaf on the surface of still water.
You do not need to hold it tightly or repeat it constantly.
It is simply present, a quiet reminder of why you are here.

Now let yourself begin to sink beneath the surface of thought.
Thoughts will continue to move across the mind like clouds across a sky.
You do not need to stop them, fight them, or follow them.
When you notice yourself caught in a thought, gently return to the sacred word.

The return is always gentle, never forceful, never frustrated.
Each return is an act of consent, a small yes to the silence beneath the noise.
It does not matter how many times you return; each one is a gift.
The practice is not about achieving silence but about choosing it, again and again.

Feel the body breathing, slow and steady, while the mind gradually quiets.
The sacred word floats in the heart, asking for nothing, offering everything.
Below the word, below the breath, below the thoughts, there is a vast stillness.
This stillness has been here all along, waiting beneath the surface of your busy life.

Let yourself descend into that stillness like sinking into warm water.
The deeper you go, the quieter it becomes, and the safer it feels.
There is a presence here that knows you completely and holds you without condition.
You do not have to name it or define it; you only have to rest in it.

If the mind begins to analyze or question this experience, that is just another thought.
Gently return to the sacred word, and let the word carry you back to stillness.
The word is a doorway, not a destination; it leads you to the silence beyond all words.
Trust the process, even when it feels like nothing is happening.

Something is always happening in this kind of prayer, even when you cannot feel it.
The deep roots of a tree grow in darkness, unseen, unfelt, but essential.
Your willingness to sit here in silence is the growth itself.
The fruits will appear in their own time, in ways you may not expect.

Now let even the sacred word begin to fade, if it wants to.
You may find yourself in a place where no word is needed at all.
Just presence, just stillness, just the quiet hum of being alive.
This is the deepest room in the house of the heart, where silence speaks.

Rest here without agenda or expectation.
Let the silence hold you the way the ocean holds a drifting boat.
You do not have to steer; you do not have to know where you are going.
The current of grace carries everything that surrenders to it.

If emotions arise, let them pass through like weather moving across a valley.
Joy may come, or sadness, or a strange tenderness without a name.
None of these are obstacles; they are the heart opening in the presence of the sacred.
Let them be, and return to the stillness that holds them all.

Notice how the body feels when the mind is quiet.
There may be a warmth in the chest, a softness in the belly, a tingling in the hands.
The body responds to deep stillness the way dry ground responds to rain.
It absorbs what it needs, gratefully and without hurry.

You have been resting in the presence of something vast and loving.
Even if you could not feel it clearly, it was here, holding you.
Your willingness to sit in silence is itself a form of prayer.
The heart knows what the mind cannot understand.

Now very gently, let the sacred word return if it has faded.
Hold it softly in your awareness, a bridge between the deep stillness and the surface world.
Begin to notice sounds around you, the room, the world beyond the room.
Let them arrive without judgment, as if they too were part of the prayer.

Feel your body again, the weight, the warmth, the breath moving in and out.
Let your fingers move slightly, and your toes.
There is no rush to leave this place; the stillness will stay with you.
It lives in the heart, always available, always waiting for your return.

Take one more breath, deep and slow, carrying gratitude for this time of quiet.
And as you open your eyes, let the world appear fresh, as if seen for the first time.
The sacred word remains in your heart, a companion for the hours ahead.
Carry this stillness gently into whatever comes next.
