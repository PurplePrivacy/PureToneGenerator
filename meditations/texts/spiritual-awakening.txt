Find a comfortable position and allow the body to be still.
Let your eyes close gently, or soften your gaze toward the ground.
Take a slow, full breath in, feeling the belly and chest expand together.
And let it go completely, allowing the body to settle with gravity.

Feel the weight of your body pressing into whatever surface supports you.
Notice the places where your skin meets the world, clothing, cushion, air.
You are here, in this body, in this room, in this moment.
Let yourself be fully arrived before going anywhere else.

Bring your attention to the soles of your feet and feel the ground beneath them.
Even if you are lying down, imagine roots extending from your feet into the earth.
Feel the solidity and patience of the ground holding you without effort.
You are anchored here, in flesh and bone and breath.

Now notice the sensations in your legs, the weight of muscle resting against itself.
Feel the bowl of the pelvis, the quiet engine room of the lower body.
Let your awareness move upward through the belly, the ribcage, the shoulders.
Feel the whole body as a warm, breathing column of aliveness.

Notice the top of your head, and imagine a gentle opening there, like a skylight.
Through that opening, your awareness can expand upward while your body stays grounded.
You do not have to leave the body to expand; the body is where you begin.
Every great journey starts with the feet firmly on the ground.

Now let your awareness gently widen to fill the room you are in.
Without opening your eyes, sense the walls, the ceiling, the floor.
Feel the quality of the air, the temperature, the sounds near and far.
You are aware of this space as easily as you are aware of your own breath.

Let your awareness expand a little further, through the walls, into the building.
Sense the other rooms, the hallways, the spaces where life happens around you.
You do not need to see them clearly, just feel the presence of the larger space.
Your awareness is gentle and curious, touching everything lightly.

Now let it expand further still, out through the walls and into the open air.
Feel the sky above the building, wide and unhurried.
Sense the street, the trees, the birds moving through the air.
Your awareness spreads like light, illuminating without grasping.

Expand outward through the neighborhood, the parks and houses and roads.
Feel the thousands of lives being lived in this area right now.
People eating, working, sleeping, laughing, worrying, dreaming.
You are part of this web of life, and your awareness can hold it all gently.

Let your awareness widen further to include the whole city or region.
Feel the rivers and hills and buildings and highways.
All of it humming with activity, with movement, with the quiet pulse of existence.
You do not need to understand it all, only to feel that it is here.

Expand now to feel the whole planet, this blue sphere turning in darkness.
Feel the oceans, vast and deep, covering most of the surface.
Feel the continents, the ice caps, the jungles, the cities glowing at night.
Eight billion lives, and countless creatures, all sharing this one home.

Notice how it feels to hold the whole earth in your awareness.
There may be tenderness, or awe, or a quiet sense of belonging.
This planet made you from its own materials, its water, its minerals, its air.
You are not separate from the earth; you are the earth, aware of itself.

Now let your awareness expand beyond the atmosphere, into the silence of space.
Feel the moon circling nearby, faithful and silver and quiet.
The sun pouring its warmth across ninety-three million miles of nothing.
Planets turning in their orbits, each one a world of mystery.

Expand further, past the outer planets, past the edge of the solar system.
Stars appear in every direction, billions of them, each one a sun.
The Milky Way spirals around you, a river of light hundreds of thousands of light-years wide.
And beyond it, other galaxies, stretching into distances the mind cannot grasp.

Let yourself float in this vastness for a moment without needing to understand it.
You are a point of awareness in an infinite field of being.
The same atoms that form distant stars are woven into your bones right now.
You are made of the universe, and the universe is aware through you.

Feel the profound stillness of cosmic space, the silence between the stars.
This silence is not empty; it is full of potential, of dark matter, of forces unseen.
In the same way, the stillness inside you is not empty either.
It is alive with presence, with the quiet hum of consciousness itself.

Now very gently, begin to draw your awareness back toward home.
Feel the galaxy condensing, the solar system appearing, the blue earth growing larger.
See the continents, the country, the city, the neighborhood.
You are returning, but you are not the same as when you left.

Feel the building around you, the room, the air on your skin.
Feel the body, warm and solid, still breathing, still beating.
Your feet are on the ground, your lungs are full of air.
You have traveled to the edge of the cosmos and returned in a single breath.

Notice what is different now, how this room feels, how this body feels.
Perhaps there is a sense of spaciousness that was not here before.
Perhaps the small concerns of the day feel lighter against such a vast backdrop.
You carry cosmic perspective now, in a body made of earth and stars.

Take a slow breath in, filling the body with this expanded awareness.
And as you release it, let yourself smile softly at the miracle of being here.
You are vast and you are small and both of those things are beautiful.
When you are ready, let your eyes open gently, and return to the day carrying this light.
