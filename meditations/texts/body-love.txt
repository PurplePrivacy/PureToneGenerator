Settle into stillness and let your body be fully supported.
There is nothing to achieve here, only something to feel.
Let your breath find its own rhythm, easy and unforced.

Begin by bringing your attention down to your feet.
These two faithful companions have carried you everywhere you have ever gone.
They have walked you into rooms full of strangers and back to the safety of home.
Thank your feet for every step they have given you without complaint.

Now bring your awareness to your ankles and lower legs.
They have climbed stairs, walked through sand, steadied you on uneven ground.
Allow a wave of appreciation to settle into your calves and shins.

Move your attention upward to your knees.
Your knees have absorbed impact and provided flexibility for your entire life.
They have knelt in gardens, crouched to meet a child's eyes, bent in prayer.
Thank your knees for their quiet, essential service.

Feel into your thighs, those powerful pillars of your body.
They carry you up hills and hold you steady when you stand.
Let yourself feel genuine admiration for their enduring strength.

Bring your awareness now to your hips and pelvis.
This is the cradle of your body, the bowl that holds your center of gravity.
Feel the deep muscles there and offer them your thanks.

Move your attention to your belly, that soft and sensitive center.
Your belly has digested every meal you have ever eaten.
It has turned food into life energy with tireless precision.
Let your appreciation flow there like warm water.

Bring your awareness to your chest and the ribs that protect your heart and lungs.
Feel the gentle expansion with each breath, the ribs opening like quiet wings.
Appreciate this elegant structure that shelters your most vital organs.

Now let your attention rest on your heart.
Your heart began beating before you were born and has not stopped since.
It has pumped blood through thousands of miles of vessels without rest.
Let yourself be moved by the devotion of your own heart.

Feel your lungs, those two soft bellows that draw life in and release it.
Every inhale brings oxygen to nourish your cells.
Every exhale releases what your body no longer needs.
Thank your lungs for breathing you through every moment of your life.

Bring your attention to your shoulders.
Your shoulders have carried bags and children and the weight of worry.
They have reached and embraced the people you love.
Let them soften now as you acknowledge everything they have borne.

Now bring your awareness to your hands.
Your hands are among the most extraordinary instruments in nature.
They have written letters, prepared food, wiped tears, and offered comfort.
Feel the intelligence that lives in your palms and fingertips.

Move your attention to your throat and neck.
Your neck holds your head with patient, tireless balance.
Feel the vulnerability and strength that live together in this narrow passage.

Bring your awareness to your face.
Your lips have smiled, kissed, and formed the sounds of your language.
Your eyes have shown you every beautiful thing you have ever seen.
Your ears have carried music and laughter and birdsong into your life.
Let gratitude settle gently around each of these faithful senses.

Now let your awareness expand to hold your entire body at once.
Feel the wholeness of this living system that carries you through the world.
Every part works with every other part in constant, quiet cooperation.

Your body has healed cuts and fought illness and adapted to change.
It has done this without instruction, without complaint, without recognition.
The intelligence of your body is older than language and deeper than thought.

Place your hands gently on your body wherever feels right.
Let this touch be an act of kindness toward yourself.
You are a body, whole and alive, worthy of love exactly as you are.

It does not matter what your body looks like or what it cannot do.
What matters is that it is here, breathing, alive, carrying you forward.
You can return to this gratitude anytime, with a single conscious breath.

When you are ready, let your eyes open gently.
Carry this tenderness for your body into the hours ahead.
Your body is your most loyal companion, and it is listening.
