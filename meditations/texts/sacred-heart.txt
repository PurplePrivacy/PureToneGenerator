Allow your body to settle into a comfortable position wherever you are right now.
Let your eyes close softly, or rest your gaze downward on a single point.
Take one slow breath in through the nose, feeling your ribcage gently expand.
And release that breath through the mouth with a quiet sigh.

Notice how the body already knows how to breathe without your effort.
Let that knowing carry you deeper into stillness for a moment.
There is nothing you need to fix or change right now.
You are simply arriving here, in the center of your own life.

Now bring your attention gently to the center of your chest.
You might place a hand there if that feels right, palm flat against the sternum.
Feel the warmth of your palm meeting the warmth of your body.
Underneath your hand, something is beating, steady and alive.

Notice the rhythm of your heart without trying to change it.
It has been beating since before you were born, faithful and tireless.
Feel that pulse as a kind of music the body makes just by being alive.
Each beat is a soft drum, keeping time with your existence.

Let your attention rest right there, in that gentle drumming.
Imagine your awareness becoming very small, entering the heart itself.
You are sitting inside a warm chamber that pulses with soft, golden light.
Each beat sends a wave of warmth outward through your whole body.

Feel that warmth traveling from the heart down through both arms to the fingertips.
Feel it moving upward through the throat and softening the muscles of the face.
Feel it spreading down through the belly, the hips, the legs, all the way to the soles of your feet.
Your entire body is being bathed in the warmth your own heart creates.

Rest here for a moment, held by this rhythmic warmth.
There is a deep intelligence in this organ that has never stopped working for you.
It does not ask permission and it does not require your attention to keep going.
And yet when you give it attention, something softens and opens.

Now imagine the space around your heart growing slightly larger.
As if the heart center were a room, and the walls gently moved outward.
This is the heartspace, a place of openness and quiet reception.
In this space, feelings can arise without needing to be named or solved.

Let whatever is present in your emotional life simply be here.
You do not need to push anything away or pull anything closer.
The heart is wide enough to hold tenderness and strength at the same time.
It can hold grief and gratitude in the same breath without contradiction.

Notice how this space feels when you simply allow it to be open.
There may be a gentle ache, or a surprising sweetness, or simply stillness.
Whatever you find here is welcome, because the heart does not judge what it holds.
It only beats, and opens, and offers warmth to everything it touches.

Now let your awareness expand gently outward from your heart center.
Feel the air around your body, the space of the room you are in.
Somewhere nearby, other hearts are beating in other chests.
Each one keeping its own rhythm, its own quiet music.

Imagine you could feel the heartbeat of someone you love.
Not hearing it, but sensing it, the way you sense warmth from a fire across a room.
There is a thread of connection between your heart and theirs that needs no words.
It exists in the shared rhythm of being alive at the same time, on the same earth.

Now let that sense of connection expand a little further.
Feel the countless hearts beating in your neighborhood, your city, across the land.
Each one is a small lamp of warmth, pulsing in the darkness.
Together they form a vast web of rhythm, a constellation of living warmth.

Animals carry beating hearts too, birds and dogs and whales in deep water.
Every creature with a heartbeat shares this one thing with you.
The rhythm of life itself, expressed in billions of small, faithful drums.
You are part of this immense chorus of beating hearts.

Let yourself feel held by that belonging for a moment.
You did not have to earn your place in this chorus.
Your heart began beating as a gift, and it has never stopped giving.
You are woven into the fabric of life by the simple fact of your pulse.

Now gently bring your awareness back to your own chest.
Feel your hand there, if you placed it, warm and steady.
Feel the heartbeat underneath, perhaps a little slower now, a little softer.
The heart responds to attention the way a flower responds to sunlight.

Take one more breath into the heart center, imagining it glowing with warmth.
And as you release that breath, let the glow remain, soft and steady.
This warmth is always here, always available, always beating for you.
You can return to this heartspace at any time simply by placing your attention here.

Begin to notice the sounds around you, the room, the air, the world outside.
Feel the weight of your body again, solid and grounded and real.
Let your fingers move gently, and your toes.
When you are ready, open your eyes slowly, carrying the warmth of the heart with you.
