Sit with your back straight and your body completely relaxed.
Place your hands on your knees with your palms facing down.
Close your eyes and let the world outside grow distant and soft.
Take a few deep breaths, letting each one draw you deeper into yourself.
You are about to explore the most ancient of all sacred sounds.

The sound OM is said to be the vibration from which all creation emerged.
It is not a word with a meaning but a frequency with a presence.
You do not need to believe anything special for this practice to work.
You need only listen, feel, and allow the vibration to move through you.

Begin by sitting in silence and listening to the room around you.
Beneath the obvious sounds, there is a hum, a subtle background frequency.
Some call it the sound of silence, the tone that exists beneath all tones.
Let your ears reach for it, even if you cannot quite hear it.

Now bring your awareness to your belly, the area below your navel.
This is where the first component of OM originates.
The sound begins as a wide, open vibration, like the letter A.
Imagine this sound being born in the depths of your abdomen.

Simply imagine the vibration of A filling your lower body.
It is a low, open tone, like the hum of the earth itself.

Now let the vibration rise naturally upward into your chest.
Here it transforms into the second component, the sound U.
Feel the resonance shifting from the belly into the ribcage.
The chest becomes a cathedral of sound, with the heart at its center.

Feel your sternum vibrating with this middle tone.
Your lungs resonate, your heart is bathed in the vibration.
Your entire torso is alive with the movement from A to U.

Now let the sound continue its journey into the head.
Here it becomes the third component, the sound M.
The M is a closed, humming sound, like the buzz of a bee in a garden.
Feel the vibration reaching your throat and rising into the skull.

Feel the M vibrating in your sinuses, your nasal passages, your cheekbones.
The bones of your face resonate like the body of a stringed instrument.
The vibration rises to the crown of your head and fills the entire skull.
The M sound seems to thin out and dissolve into something beyond sound.

Now hold all three components together in your awareness.
A in the belly, U in the chest, M in the head.
They form one continuous arc of vibration from root to crown.
This is OM, not three sounds but one sound with three expressions.

Imagine the complete OM vibrating silently through your whole body.
It begins deep in the belly and sweeps upward through chest and head.
Then it dissolves into silence at the crown, like a wave breaking on a shore.
That silence after OM is the most sacred part of the sound.

Rest in that silence for a moment.
It is not empty but full, pregnant with potential.
Every OM dissolves into this silence, and every new OM is born from it.
Feel the intimate relationship between vibration and stillness.

Now let another wave of OM arise from the belly.
A opens wide in the lower body, spreading warmth and grounding.
U rises and swells in the chest, filling the heart with resonance.
M ascends and hums through the skull, tingling at the crown.
Silence follows, vast and welcoming.

With each cycle, the vibration penetrates deeper into your cells.
Your bones begin to feel the hum, as if they are tuning forks.
Your organs absorb the frequency and vibrate at a subtler level.
You are becoming a body made of vibration, resonating from the inside out.

Let the OM continue to cycle at its own pace.
You are not driving it but riding it, like a leaf on a slow river.
Trust the intelligence of the vibration itself.
It has been doing this work for longer than human beings have existed.

Notice how the mind grows quieter with each cycle.
Thoughts find less traction when the body is full of vibration.
There is nothing to think about when every cell is singing.
The mind rests, not because it is forced to, but because it has found something better.

Feel the vibration now as a presence rather than a sound.
The universe itself vibrates, and you are tuning yourself to its frequency.
Your heartbeat is a drum, your breath is a wind instrument, your bones are chimes.
You are an orchestra playing a single, infinite note.

Let the OM grow softer and softer with each cycle.
The vibration becomes more subtle, more interior, less like sound and more like light.
The belly glows with a warm amber light as A passes through.
The chest fills with a golden radiance as U resonates.
The head illuminates with a white brilliance as M vibrates and dissolves.

Now let the last OM dissolve completely into silence.
Feel the vibration slowly fading from your cells like the last ring of a bell.
The silence that remains is not ordinary silence.
It is alive, awake, and luminous.

This silence was here before the first OM and will be here after the last.
It is the ground of all experience, the canvas on which all sound is painted.
There is nothing to do, nothing to become, nothing to understand.
Only this silence, holding everything, needing nothing.

Stay here for a few more breaths, resting in the aftermath of vibration.
Your body feels cleansed and tuned, like an instrument after careful adjustment.
Your heart is open and quiet, beating in rhythm with something vast.
You are complete, exactly as you are, in this moment of silence.

When you are ready, take a deeper breath and feel the room around you.
The vibration of OM continues at a level too subtle to hear.
It will accompany you through the rest of your day like a quiet companion.
Open your eyes gently and return, carrying the silence within you.
