Allow yourself to arrive fully in this moment, just as you are.
There is no preparation needed and nothing to fix before we begin.
Let your eyes close and feel the weight of your body settling.
Take a slow breath in through your nose and release it softly.
Whatever you are carrying right now, you are welcome to bring it here.
This practice is a way of turning toward difficulty with great kindness.

We will move through four steps, each one a different way of being present.
The letters R, A, I, and N form a path through whatever you are facing.
Let us begin.

Bring to mind something that has been difficult for you recently.
It does not need to be the hardest thing in your life right now.
Choose something that is present enough to feel but not overwhelming.
Perhaps a conflict with someone, or a worry that keeps returning.
Let it come into your awareness gently, like opening a door slightly.

Now we begin with R, recognizing what is here.
Simply name what you are experiencing in this moment.
You might say silently to yourself, I am feeling anxious right now.
Naming what is happening creates a small space between you and the experience.
You are not the emotion, you are the one who notices the emotion.

Notice where in your body this difficulty lives right now.
Perhaps there is a tightness in your chest or a heaviness in your belly.
Perhaps your throat feels constricted or your shoulders are creeping upward.
Simply recognize these physical sensations without trying to change them.
Say to yourself, yes, this is what is happening right now.

Now we move to A, allowing what is here to simply be here.
Allowing does not mean you approve of the difficulty or want it to stay.
It means you stop fighting against the fact that it is already here.
Imagine making space around the difficulty, like a vast sky around a cloud.
The sky does not push the cloud away or try to dissolve it.
The sky simply holds it, with room to spare on every side.

Say to yourself gently, I can let this be here for now.
Feel what happens in your body when you stop resisting.
Perhaps the tightness in your chest softens just slightly.
Allowing is not passive, it takes great courage to stop fighting.
You are choosing to be with what is rather than running from it.

Now we move to I, investigating with kindness and gentle curiosity.
Turn toward the difficulty the way you would turn toward a friend in pain.
Not with analysis or judgment, but with warm, open interest.
Ask yourself softly, what does this feeling really need right now.
What is the belief or the fear that lives at the center of this feeling.

Bring your attention to the strongest sensation in your body right now.
Place your hand over that area if it feels right, your chest or belly.
Feel the warmth of your own hand through your clothing.
Does the sensation have a shape, a color, a temperature.
Is there a younger part of you that is holding this pain.
What would that part of you most need to hear right now.

This investigation is not intellectual, it happens through your body.
Let whatever arises come without censoring or judging it.
Every sensation and emotion is information, not a problem to solve.

Now we arrive at N, nurturing yourself with natural awareness.
Having recognized, allowed, and investigated, you now offer yourself care.
What does the most tender part of you need to hear right now.
Perhaps it is simply, you are not alone in this.
Perhaps it is, this is hard and you are doing your best.
Perhaps it is, I am here with you and I am not leaving.

Place both hands over your heart if that feels comforting.
Feel the warmth and the gentle pressure of your own touch.
You are holding yourself the way a loving parent holds a child.
Say to yourself, I care about this suffering and I wish you peace.

Feel what happens in your body when you offer yourself this tenderness.
Perhaps warmth spreads from your heart outward through your torso.
This is your nervous system responding to your own compassion.
You have the power to soothe yourself simply by being kind.

Now let the specific difficulty begin to fade from the foreground.
You do not push it away, you simply let your attention widen.
It is still there but it is no longer the only thing in your awareness.
You are larger than any single feeling or experience.

Rest in this wider awareness for a few moments.
Feel your whole body sitting here, breathing, alive, present.
You turned toward pain instead of away from it.
You met yourself with recognition, permission, curiosity, and love.

This practice is available to you anytime difficulty arises.
Each time you practice, you strengthen your ability to be with what is.
Take one more breath and carry this tenderness with you into your day.
Whatever comes, you now know how to meet it with an open heart.
