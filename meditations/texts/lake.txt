Let your body settle into a comfortable position and close your eyes.
Feel the places where your body meets the surface beneath you.
Allow your breathing to find its own natural, easy rhythm.
There is nothing you need to do right now except be here.

Imagine yourself sitting beside a beautiful lake at dawn.
The lake stretches out before you, wide and calm and clear.
Its surface is so still that it reflects everything around it perfectly.
You can see the sky mirrored in the water, cloud for cloud.
You can see the trees along the shore reflected upside down.
The mountains in the distance appear twice, above and below.

Take a moment to notice the colors of this place.
The deep blue of the water meeting the pale blue of the sky.
The dark green of pine trees standing along the shore.

Now imagine that you are this lake.
Your body is the body of water, resting in the earth.
Your edges are the shoreline, clearly defined and held.
Your surface is open to the sky above, receiving everything.
Your depths extend far below what anyone can see from the surface.

In this moment, your lake surface is perfectly still.
There is no wind and the water is like a mirror.
This is what your mind feels like when it is at rest.
Clear and open and reflecting things just as they are.

Now a gentle breeze begins to move across the water.
Small ripples form on the surface and the reflections shimmer.
The mirror image breaks apart into dancing fragments of color.
The breeze is like a passing thought or a mild emotion.
It touches the surface and creates a small disturbance.
But notice how the depths of the lake remain completely still.
Just a few feet below the rippling surface, the water is calm.

The breeze grows stronger and the ripples become small waves.
The surface is now choppy and the reflections are lost entirely.
You cannot see the sky or the mountains in the water anymore.
This is what it feels like when emotions stir more strongly.
The surface of your experience becomes turbulent and unclear.

But even now, the depths of the lake remain undisturbed.
Far below the waves, the water sits in perfect darkness and stillness.
Fish move slowly through the deep, unbothered by the surface weather.
Stones rest on the bottom exactly where they have always been.
The deep water does not know or care what the surface is doing.

You have these same depths within you right now.
Beneath the thoughts that ripple across the surface of your mind.
Beneath the emotions that sometimes churn and toss.
There is a place in you that is always still and clear.
It is not something you need to create or earn or find.
It is already there, waiting for you to sink down into it.

Now imagine a heavy rain beginning to fall on the lake.
Thousands of raindrops strike the surface all at once.
Each drop creates its own small circle of disturbance.
The surface is alive with splashing, pocking, overlapping rings.
This is what it feels like when many things happen at once.
When worries pile on worries and the mind cannot find peace.

And still the depths are quiet and still and dark.
The rain does not reach down to where the water is deep.
Nothing that happens on the surface can penetrate to the bottom.
You can rest in your own depths even when life feels overwhelming.
You can let the storm happen on the surface without drowning in it.

The rain slows and then stops altogether.
The clouds part and warm sunlight falls on the water.
Watch how quickly the lake returns to stillness when the disturbance passes.
The ripples fade and the surface smooths itself out naturally.
Within minutes the water is calm and clear and mirror-like again.
This is the natural tendency of your own mind as well.
When you stop adding to the disturbance, peace returns on its own.

Now the lake is still again and you can see into the water.
The clarity reaches down through layers of pale green light.
You can see the sandy bottom and the smooth dark stones.
This is what it is like to know yourself clearly.

You are this lake in every moment of your life.
Sometimes the surface is perfectly still and you feel at peace.
Sometimes the wind blows and you feel unsettled and stirred.
Sometimes storms rage and you feel battered and overwhelmed.
But your depths are always there, always still, always available.

You do not need to stop the wind or prevent the rain.
You only need to remember that you are more than your surface.
You are the whole lake, from the surface down to the floor.

Let yourself sink down into your own still depths right now.
Feel the quiet that lives beneath all the movement of your life.
You can return here anytime by simply remembering the lake.
Rest here for a moment, in your own undisturbed stillness.
Breathe gently and know that this peace is always yours.
