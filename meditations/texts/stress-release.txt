Allow your body to come to rest.
Let go of any effort to hold yourself in a particular way.
Simply let your body be as it is, right now, in this moment.

Close your eyes and take a slow breath in.
Hold it for just a moment at the top.
Then let it go with a long, soft exhale.
Let your breath return to its natural rhythm.

Begin by bringing your attention to the top of your head and your scalp.
Sometimes stress gathers in the muscles of the scalp without our knowing.
Imagine warm water flowing across your head, softening everything it touches.

Move your attention to your forehead.
This is a place where worry often lives.
Imagine the tension there as a thin layer of ice.
Now imagine your breath as warm water flowing over that ice.
The forehead smooths and softens as the ice melts.

Bring your awareness to the space around your eyes.
The small muscles there work constantly throughout the day.
Let them release now, all of them, completely.
Feel your eyes resting in their sockets like smooth stones in warm sand.

Now notice your jaw.
The jaw is one of the most common places to store stress.
Let your jaw drop slightly, allowing a small gap between your teeth.
Imagine warm water flowing along the hinge of your jaw, melting the tension.
Your entire face becomes a landscape of ease.

Move your attention to your throat and neck.
Imagine the warm current flowing down through your neck, dissolving tightness.
Your neck muscles lengthen and soften.
Any strain from the day dissolves.

Now bring your full attention to your shoulders.
These are the shelves where stress most loves to accumulate.
Let them drop, consciously and completely, away from your ears.

Imagine the ice of tension on your shoulders beginning to melt.
Warm water flows across the tops of your shoulders and down your arms.
Feel the extraordinary relief of shoulders that have finally let go.

The warm current continues through your upper arms and elbows.
It flows through your forearms and into your wrists.
The warm water reaches your hands and flows out through your fingertips.
Every bit of tension from your shoulders has been carried away.

Now bring your awareness to your upper back.
The muscles between your shoulder blades often hold deep, stubborn tension.
Imagine the warmth penetrating into those muscles, layer by layer.
Your upper back widens and softens.

Move your attention to your chest.
Stress often constricts the chest, making breath shallow.
Imagine the warm current flowing through your ribcage.
Your chest opens like a window being unlatched.
There is more room for your lungs, more room for your heart.

Now bring your awareness to your belly.
The belly is where anxiety often settles.
Imagine the warm water swirling slowly through your abdomen.
Your abdominal muscles soften and release.
Your belly becomes a place of ease rather than worry.

Move to your lower back, that faithful bridge.
Imagine the warmth flowing into those muscles like a hot spring.
Your lower back releases its habitual tension.
There is a sense of length and openness along the spine.

Bring your attention to your hips and pelvis.
Imagine the warm current flowing into the bowl of your pelvis.
Your hips release and widen slightly.

Now feel the warmth moving down through your thighs.
The warm water flows through them, dissolving any remaining ice.
Your thighs become heavy and warm, deeply relaxed.

The current moves through your knees and down through your calves.
It flows into your ankles and fills your feet.
Any tension remaining is carried out through the soles of your feet into the earth.

Now feel your entire body as one unified field of relaxation.
Every muscle has softened from head to toe.
The ice of stress has been replaced by the warmth of release.

This is what your body feels like without stress.
This is your natural state when tension is not present.
Your body remembers this state and it can return here again.

You can scan your body, find the ice, and breathe warm water through it.
Each time you practice, the release comes more easily.

Take one more deep, easy breath.
Feel the fullness of relaxation in every part of your body.
When you are ready, open your eyes slowly and gently.
Carry this softness with you into the rest of your day.
