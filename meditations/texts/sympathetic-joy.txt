Settle into a comfortable position and let the body become still.
Feel the warmth of your own skin, the quiet heat the body generates just by being alive.
Place your attention on the center of the chest, where that warmth is most concentrated.
Let your eyes close, and take a slow, full breath into that warm center.

This warmth is yours, reliable and constant, the body's gift to itself.
Feel it spreading gently outward from the heart, through the shoulders, down the arms.
Let it soften the belly, the hips, the legs.
You are sitting in your own warmth, and it asks nothing of you.

Today you are going to practice a very particular kind of happiness.
It is the happiness that arises when you witness someone else's good fortune.
This happiness is called sympathetic joy, or mudita, and it is one of the heart's natural abilities.
Like a muscle, it grows stronger with practice.

Begin by calling to mind a child you know, or any young person full of life.
Picture this child laughing, running, completely absorbed in the joy of being alive.
See the brightness in their eyes, the carelessness of their movement.
Let yourself feel the natural delight that arises when you watch a child play.

Notice what happens in your body as you hold this image.
Perhaps a warmth in the chest, a softening of the face, a small smile forming.
This is mudita, joy that does not cost you anything and has no limit.
You are not losing anything by feeling happy for this child; you are gaining something.

Whisper inwardly: may your happiness continue and grow.
May your laughter ring out for many years to come.
May the world be kind to you, and may you always know this freedom.
Let these wishes strengthen the warmth already present in your chest.

Now bring to mind someone you love deeply, a partner, a friend, a family member.
Think of a time when something wonderful happened to them, a success, a celebration, a dream fulfilled.
Remember their face when they told you the good news, the light in their expression.
Let yourself feel the joy you felt for them in that moment.

This joy is not about you; it is entirely for them, and that is what makes it so pure.
There is no envy in it, no calculation, no wondering what you will receive.
It is the heart simply opening because someone you love is thriving.
Feel how spacious the chest becomes when you let this joy flow freely.

Whisper to them: I am so glad for your happiness.
Your success does not diminish me; it fills me with warmth.
May your good fortune continue, and may it bring you deep satisfaction.
Feel these words as a current of warm energy flowing from your heart to theirs.

Now expand slightly, and bring to mind someone you know but are not close to.
A coworker, a neighbor, someone you see at the market or on the street.
Imagine that something good has happened to them, a promotion, a new love, a windfall, a recovery.
See them smiling, and practice feeling glad for them, even without a personal connection.

The mind may resist slightly, wondering why you should care about a stranger's happiness.
That resistance is normal; just notice it without judgment.
And then choose to feel glad anyway, because joy shared is joy multiplied.

Whisper to this person: I am happy that things are going well for you.
You deserve good fortune, as all beings do.
May your happiness ripple outward and touch others.
Notice if the warmth in your chest responds, even faintly.

Now, if you are willing, bring to mind someone you find difficult.
This does not have to be your greatest adversary, just someone who triggers discomfort.
Imagine that something genuinely good has happened in their life.
Perhaps they received recognition, or reconciled with someone, or found peace.

Notice what arises in the body. There may be contraction, resistance, even a flash of resentment.
This is honest, and there is no shame in it.
The practice is not about forcing feelings but about gently expanding the heart's capacity.
See if you can find even a tiny willingness to be glad for this person.

Even a small drop of sympathetic joy is meaningful here.
Whisper: even though this is hard, I wish you well.
Your happiness does not take anything from me.
I choose to practice being glad, even imperfectly.

Feel whatever arises without judgment, whether it is warmth or resistance or a mix of both.
The heart learns to stretch through practice, not through force.
This is patient work, and you are doing it simply by being willing to try.

Now release all specific people from your awareness and feel your own heart.
Notice its condition right now, perhaps warmer, perhaps more open, perhaps tired.
Whatever you find, let it be exactly as it is.
The practice of sympathetic joy is also a practice of self-compassion.

Because when you can be happy for others without envy, you are freeing yourself.
When you rejoice in another's joy, your own capacity for happiness doubles and triples.
You are no longer limited to only the good things that happen to you personally.
This is not a fantasy; it is a possibility that begins in your own chest.

Let yourself feel the goodness of this practice, simple and ancient and powerful.
That choice to feel glad for others has changed something in the landscape of your heart.
The grooves of joy have been deepened; the pathways of delight have been strengthened.

Take a slow breath into the heart center and feel whatever warmth remains.
This warmth is your own happiness, expanded by the happiness of others.
It does not run out; it grows the more you give it away.

Now gently bring your attention back to the body, the room, the sounds around you.
Feel the weight of your limbs, the texture of the surface beneath you.
Let your fingers move, and your toes, and take one more slow, deep breath.
When you are ready, open your eyes, and carry this practice of glad-heartedness into the world.
