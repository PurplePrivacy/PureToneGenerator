Find a comfortable position and let your body settle completely.
Feel the warmth of the air on your skin, gentle and unhurried.
Let your eyes close and allow the world outside to grow quiet.

Bring your attention to the rhythm of your breathing.
Feel the soft rise of your chest as you inhale.
Feel the slow, easy release as you exhale.
You are safe here, held by this moment, held by your own attention.

Let your awareness travel gently to the center of your chest.
Notice the warmth that gathers there, steady and alive.
This warmth is your own tenderness, waiting to be offered.

Now allow your mind to drift back in time, softly, without effort.
Imagine yourself walking down a quiet hallway of memory.
The light is warm and golden, and every door you pass holds a moment from your past.
You are looking for one door in particular, the one that leads to your younger self.

When you find it, open it gently and step through.
You find yourself in a place from your childhood, somewhere that feels familiar.
It might be a room, a garden, a kitchen, a quiet corner where you used to sit.
Take a moment to notice the details, the colors, the textures, the quality of light.

And there, in this place, you see your younger self.
A small version of you, at whatever age feels right in this moment.
Notice what they are wearing, how they are sitting or standing.
Notice the expression on their face.

Approach them slowly, the way you would approach a shy animal in the wild.
Let them see you coming, let them feel your calm presence before you speak.
Kneel down so that your eyes are level with theirs.

Look into their face and recognize yourself.
This is you, the original you, before the world asked you to be anything else.
They may look uncertain or tired or brave or sad.
Whatever you see, let yourself feel it fully without turning away.

Now speak to this child in the kindest voice you have ever used.
Tell them that you have come from their future and that they are going to be okay.
Tell them that the hard things they are going through will not last forever.
Let them hear this from someone who knows it to be true.

If this child is carrying something heavy, ask them to tell you about it.
Listen without interrupting, without correcting, without rushing.
Their feelings are real and they deserve to be witnessed.
You are the safe adult they needed then, and you are here now.

Reach out and take their small hand in yours if they will let you.
Feel the warmth of their fingers, the softness of their skin.
Tell them that they did nothing wrong, that they are not broken.
Tell them that they are good, all the way through, exactly as they are.

If they are afraid, wrap your arms around them gently.
Feel their small body relax against your chest as they begin to trust you.
Hold them the way you always wished someone would hold you.
Let them feel the steady beating of your grown-up heart against their ear.

Whisper to them that they are loved, that they have always been loved.
Tell them that the loneliness they felt was not the truth about them.
The truth is that they are worthy of warmth and belonging.
The truth is that they matter, simply because they exist.

Now imagine this child beginning to smile, slowly, like sunrise.
See the light return to their eyes as they believe what you are saying.
They have been waiting so long for someone to come back for them.
And you came, you are here, you did not forget.

Ask this child if there is anything they want to do together.
Perhaps they want to play, or draw, or simply sit quietly in the sun.
Give them whatever they need, without hurry, without agenda.
This time belongs to both of you.

When the moment feels complete, tell your younger self that you are taking them with you.
They do not have to stay in this old place anymore.
Imagine them standing up and placing their hand in yours.
Feel the warmth of that small hand, trusting and ready.

Begin to walk together back through the golden hallway toward the present.
With each step, feel the integration of young and old, past and now.
This child is not separate from you, they live inside you still.
They are the part of you that feels wonder, that loves purely, that trusts openly.

As you step back into the present moment, feel their presence settle into your chest.
They are home now, safe inside the life you have built.
Place your hand over your heart and feel them there, warm and whole.

Take a slow, deep breath and fill your whole body with this reunion.
You are more complete now than you were a few minutes ago.
The part of you that was waiting has been found and welcomed home.

Promise yourself that you will visit this child again, often and with love.
They need your attention the way a garden needs rain.
And you need their openness the way a river needs its source.

Rest here in this feeling of wholeness for as long as you wish.
You carry your younger self within you, treasured and protected.
You are both the child and the one who came back for the child.
And that is one of the bravest and most beautiful things a person can do.
