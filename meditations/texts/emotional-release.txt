Find a comfortable position and let your body rest.
Let the surface beneath you hold you completely.
Close your eyes and give yourself permission to be exactly as you are.
Whatever you are feeling right now is welcome in this space.

Begin with a few slow, easy breaths.
Let each inhale arrive without effort.
Let each exhale carry you a little deeper into stillness.

Bring your attention to your body and notice its overall state.
Are there areas of tightness, heaviness, or warmth?
Simply notice, the way you might notice clouds in a sky.

Now imagine that inside your body there is a safe, warm space.
It might be in your chest, your belly, or somewhere else entirely.
This is a container where any emotion can be held.
It is lined with warmth and softness.
Nothing that enters it will be judged or rejected.

With gentle curiosity, invite whatever emotion is present to come forward.
You do not need to name it or understand it.
Simply notice if something is there, waiting to be acknowledged.

If an emotion comes forward, welcome it.
Say silently, I see you, and you are welcome here.
This is not about fixing or changing anything.

Notice where in your body this emotion lives.
It may be a tightness in the throat or a heaviness in the chest.
It may be a knot in the belly or a pressure behind the eyes.

Breathe gently into that area of your body.
Your breath does not push the emotion away; it keeps it company.
Your breath says, I am here with you, take your time.

Notice the qualities of this emotion without trying to change them.
Is it heavy or light, sharp or diffuse?
Does it pulse or remain steady?

Allow the emotion to be exactly as it is.
You do not need to make it smaller or more manageable.
Your only task is to hold it with the same tenderness you would offer a child.

If the emotion grows stronger, that is okay.
Sometimes feelings intensify when they are finally given space.
It is a sign that the emotion trusts you enough to show itself fully.

Keep breathing, slow and steady.
Your breath is an anchor that keeps you safe while the emotion moves.
You can feel deeply and remain grounded at the same time.

Notice if the emotion wants to express itself through the body.
Perhaps your jaw wants to tighten, or your hands want to clench.
Allow whatever movement feels natural, without forcing anything.

If tears come, let them come.
Tears are the body's way of releasing pressure.
Crying is a biological act of healing, as natural as breathing.
If no tears come, that is equally valid.

Continue to breathe into the area where the emotion lives.
With each breath, notice if anything is shifting.
The emotion may be changing shape, intensity, or location.
Follow it with your attention, the way you would follow a bird in flight.

Sometimes an emotion reveals another emotion beneath it.
Anger may reveal hurt, fear may reveal grief, numbness may reveal longing.
If a deeper feeling emerges, welcome it the same way.

You are choosing to feel rather than to avoid.
This is how emotions complete their natural cycle.
They arise, they are felt, and they move through.

Notice if the emotion is beginning to shift on its own.
It may be growing softer, lighter, or less urgent.
This is the natural process of release; you do not need to push it.

If the emotion feels stuck, that is also fine.
Some feelings need to be visited many times before they are ready to go.
Your willingness to be present is enough for today.

Now imagine the warmth of the safe space growing brighter.
It wraps around the emotion like a soft blanket.
You have given it the one thing it needed most: your attention.

Begin to widen your awareness back to your whole body.
Notice how the body feels now compared to when you began.

Take a slow, deep breath and let it fill your entire chest.
As you exhale, imagine any remaining tension flowing gently into the earth.

You have created a practice of allowing.
Every emotion, given enough warmth and space, will eventually move through you.

Feel the steadiness of your breath and the weight of your body.
You are here, intact, having felt something real.
Be gentle with yourself in the hours that follow.

When you are ready, let your eyes open slowly.
Notice that you are safe, that the world is still here.
Carry this willingness to feel into the rest of your day.
