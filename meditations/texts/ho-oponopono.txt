There is an ancient Hawaiian practice called Ho'oponopono.
It is a prayer of four simple phrases, spoken from the heart.
These four phrases carry the power to heal what feels broken within you.

The first phrase is I'm sorry.
The second is Please forgive me.
The third is Thank you.
The fourth is I love you.

Begin by turning these words toward yourself, toward your own tender life.
Feel the warmth of your hand resting over your heart.
Imagine speaking to the part of you that carries old wounds and quiet shame.

I'm sorry for the times I was harsh with myself when I needed gentleness.
I'm sorry for the moments I forgot my own worth and beauty.
Feel how these words soften something tight inside your chest.

Please forgive me for carrying burdens that were never mine to hold.
Please forgive me for believing I was not enough exactly as I am.
Let these words move through you like warm water dissolving old salt.

Thank you for surviving, for getting back up, for being here today.
Thank you for every breath, every heartbeat, every stubborn act of hope.
Feel gratitude glowing in the center of your chest like a small, steady flame.

I love you, all of you, the broken parts and the beautiful ones.
I love you without condition, without reservation, without limit.
Let these words soak into your bones like sunlight warming cold stone.

Rest here for a moment, letting all four phrases echo softly in your body.
I'm sorry, please forgive me, thank you, I love you.
Feel them pulsing gently with each heartbeat.

Now bring to mind someone you love, someone close to your heart.
See their face clearly, the way their eyes look when they smile at you.
Hold them gently in your awareness as you offer these healing words.

I'm sorry for any way I may have added to your suffering.
Please forgive me for the times I could not show up the way you needed.
Feel the space between you and this person grow softer and more spacious.

Thank you for your presence in my life, for all you have given me.
Thank you for the ways you have helped me grow, even through difficulty.
Let gratitude flow from your heart toward theirs like a warm current of water.

I love you, fully and freely, with all of my imperfect heart.
I love you for who you are, not for what you give me.
Feel the warmth of this love connecting you like a bridge of golden light.

Now allow another person to come to mind, perhaps someone more difficult.
Someone with whom there is tension, distance, or unresolved pain.
You do not need to force warmth, only willingness.

I'm sorry for my part in what has come between us.
Please forgive me for holding on to resentment instead of reaching for peace.
Notice any tightness in your body and breathe warmth into that place.

Thank you for what this relationship has taught me about myself.
Thank you for being a mirror, even when the reflection was hard to see.
Let whatever gratitude is possible arrive in its own time and measure.

I love you as a fellow human being, imperfect and learning, just as I am.
I love the part of you that is simply trying to find your way home.
You do not have to mean this perfectly, only sincerely.

Now widen the circle of your heart to include all beings everywhere.
Imagine your heart expanding outward like ripples on still water.
Every person on this earth carries their own invisible wounds.

I'm sorry for all the suffering in this world that I cannot see or touch.
Please forgive me for the ways I close my heart when I feel overwhelmed.
Feel the humility of these words, the gentleness of this surrender.

Thank you for the web of life that connects every living being to every other.
Thank you for the chance to be alive during this brief, precious time.
Let your gratitude become vast, as wide as the sky above you.

I love you, all of you, every person, every creature, every form of life.
I love the wholeness of existence, even the parts I do not understand.
Feel this love as a warm light radiating outward from your heart in every direction.

These words are always available to you, in any moment, for any reason.
