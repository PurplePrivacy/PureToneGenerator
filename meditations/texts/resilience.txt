Settle into a comfortable position and let your body be still.
Feel the solid surface beneath you, reliable and present.
Close your eyes and turn your attention inward.

Begin with your breath, that most faithful companion.
It has been with you through everything you have ever lived.
Every triumph and every trial, your breath was there.

Bring your awareness to your spine, that central column of your body.
It runs from the base of your pelvis to the base of your skull.
Notice its quiet strength, the way it supports you without complaint.

Now feel into your core, the muscles of your abdomen and lower back.
This is your physical center of power, where stability lives.
Feel the solidity there, the readiness, the quiet capability.

Bring your attention to your feet and feel them, real and grounded.
Your feet have walked you through every chapter of your life.
They have carried you forward even when the path was unclear.

Now begin to connect with your inner strength, not the physical kind alone.
There is a strength inside you that goes deeper than muscle and bone.
It is the part of you that keeps going when everything says stop.

Think back to a time when things were genuinely difficult.
Let the memory come forward, not to relive the pain, but to see what you did.
You faced it, you endured it, and you are here now.

Notice what qualities you drew on during that time.
Perhaps it was patience, the ability to wait out the storm.
Perhaps it was courage, or simply the refusal to give in.

Whatever quality carried you through, it is still inside you.
It is part of your character, woven into who you are.
Feel it in your body right now, as a warmth or a solidity.

Think of another time you navigated something hard.
A loss, a change, an unexpected blow.
You adapted, you found your footing, you rebuilt.

You may not have felt strong during those times.
Sometimes resilience feels like exhaustion, like barely holding on.
But holding on is itself an act of extraordinary power.

Feel that power in your body now.
It might live in your spine, your chest, your belly, your jaw.
Let yourself feel it without judgment or analysis.

Your body carries the memory of every challenge you have survived.
Not as trauma alone, but as evidence of your capacity.
Your heart has learned to break and then to keep beating.

Think of the emotional strength you have developed over your lifetime.
The ability to feel pain and not be destroyed by it.
The ability to grieve and then, slowly, to re-engage with life.

Each difficult experience taught you something about yourself.
You know more now about what you can handle than you did years ago.
That knowledge is a form of armor that no one can take from you.

Feel the steadiness that comes from knowing you have been tested.
It shows in the way you stand, the way you speak, the way you listen.
You carry that groundedness in your own body right now.

Bring your awareness to your hands.
These hands have rebuilt things that were broken.
They have reached out for help and they have offered help.

Now bring your attention to your heart.
It has closed in self-protection and then slowly opened again.
The willingness to open your heart after it has been hurt is one of the bravest things a person can do.

Each beat of your heart is an act of continuation, a vote for life.
Your heart does not deliberate; it simply keeps going.
Let that faithfulness be a mirror of your own resilience.

Now think about the challenges you are facing right now.
Look at them from this grounded, resilient place within you.
They may seem different from here, less overwhelming.

You have evidence that you can handle hard things.
Every morning you have woken up and tried again is proof.
Every time you have adapted to something you did not choose is proof.

This inner strength does not depend on feeling confident.
You can feel afraid and still be resilient.
Resilience is not the absence of struggle; it is the willingness to continue through it.

Let this willingness become a felt sense in your body.
A warmth in your core, a steadiness in your legs, a calm in your breath.
You are built to endure, to adapt, to recover, to grow.

Take a moment to honor yourself for everything you have come through.
Not with pride or ego, but with honest recognition.
You have done hard things, and you will do hard things again.
And each time, the strength will still be there, waiting.

Feel your feet on the ground, solid and connected.
Feel your heart, beating its continuous yes.
You are resilient, and you have always been.

When you are ready, open your eyes slowly.
Whatever comes next, you have what it takes to meet it.
You know this because you have met everything that came before.
