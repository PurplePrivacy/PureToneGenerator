Settle into your seat and allow your body to become still.
Close your eyes and take three slow breaths, each one deeper than the last.
Let the last exhale dissolve any urgency in your mind.
You are about to practice a subtle and powerful form of awareness.

Every moment of experience has a feeling tone, like a musical note.
That tone is pleasant, unpleasant, or neutral.
This is not emotion, not story, not judgment.
It is something more basic, the first flash of reaction before the mind interprets.

You will practice recognizing this tone without acting on it.
Pleasant without grasping, unpleasant without pushing away.
Neutral without drifting into dullness.
This is the second foundation of mindfulness, the contemplation of feeling.

Begin with the body, where feeling tones are easiest to detect.
Bring your attention to the sensations in your hands.
Notice whatever is present, warmth, tingling, pressure, or stillness.
Now ask yourself gently, is this pleasant, unpleasant, or neutral?
Do not think about the answer, simply feel it.

Move your attention to the sensations in your feet.
Feel whatever is there, the pressure of the floor, the temperature of the air.
What is the feeling tone of this experience?
Some sensations will be clearly pleasant, others clearly unpleasant.

Many sensations will be neutral, and that is perfectly fine.
Neutral is the most common and the most overlooked feeling tone.
The mind tends to skip over it in search of something more interesting.
Practice resting your attention on neutral without needing it to change.

Now bring your awareness to the sensations in your chest.
The breath moves here, and the heart beats beneath the ribs.
Is there pleasure in the expansion, or tension in the effort?
Or is it so familiar that it registers as neutral?

Scan through other areas of the body at your own pace.
The shoulders, the jaw, the space behind the eyes.
Each area carries its own tone in this moment.
This simple noticing begins to loosen the grip of automatic reactivity.

Now shift your attention from the body to sounds.
Open your ears to whatever is present in the environment.
As each sound reaches you, notice its feeling tone.
Is hearing this sound pleasant, unpleasant, or neutral?

Some sounds carry an immediate pleasantness, a quality of beauty or comfort.
Others may feel harsh, grating, or intrusive.
Most will be neither, just sounds passing through awareness.
Notice how quickly the mind wants to add a story to a sound.

Before the story begins, there was just a feeling tone.
It is the feeling tone that drives the story, not the other way around.
When you see the tone clearly, the story loses some of its power.
You gain a precious moment of freedom between stimulus and reaction.

Now bring this same awareness to your thoughts.
Notice when a thought arises in your mind.
Before you engage with its content, feel its tone.
Is the thought accompanied by a pleasant, unpleasant, or neutral feeling?

A memory of a loved one may carry a pleasant tone.
A worry about tomorrow may carry an unpleasant one.
You do not need to analyze the thought, only feel its flavor.
The flavor is in the body as much as in the mind.

Notice how pleasant-toned thoughts tend to pull you in.
The mind wants to stay with them, elaborate on them, repeat them.
This is the movement of grasping, and it begins with pleasant feeling tone.
You can see it happening without being carried away by it.

Notice how unpleasant-toned thoughts tend to push you away.
The mind wants to escape them, argue with them, or suppress them.
This is the movement of aversion, and it begins with unpleasant feeling tone.
You can see it happening without acting on it.

These movements of grasping, aversion, and ignorance shape your entire life.
But they all begin with something as simple as a feeling tone.
By seeing the tone clearly, you interrupt the chain at its very first link.
This is where liberation lives, in the gap between tone and reaction.

Return now to the body and feel whatever is most prominent.
Label its feeling tone softly in your mind.
Then let it be, without trying to keep it or push it away.
This gentle labeling is the entire practice.

Notice how the feeling tones are constantly changing.
What was pleasant a moment ago may now be neutral.
Nothing in experience is permanent, not even the simplest feeling tone.
This impermanence is not a problem but a doorway to freedom.

Rest now in open awareness, letting all sensations, sounds, and thoughts arise.
Each one carries its tone, and you notice without choosing.
There is nothing to fix, nothing to achieve, only this seeing.
The seeing itself is the medicine.

Take a few more breaths in this open, receptive awareness.
Feel how spacious the mind becomes when it stops grasping and resisting.
This spaciousness is always available to you.
Carry this gentle awareness with you as you return to your day.
