Let your body settle into stillness, slowly and without effort.
Feel the places where your body touches the surface beneath you.
Each point of contact is an anchor, holding you gently in the present.

Allow your eyes to close and your breathing to find its own natural rhythm.
There is nowhere to travel, nothing to seek, nothing to achieve.
The peace you are looking for is not somewhere else.
It is already within you, waiting beneath the surface.

Begin by noticing the outermost layer of your experience, the busy mind.
Thoughts stream past like traffic on a distant highway.
Plans, memories, judgments, questions, fragments of conversation.
This is the surface of the ocean, always moving, always churning.

You do not need to calm these waves or slow the traffic.
Simply notice the movement from a slight distance, as if watching from a hill.
The thoughts continue, but you are not inside them anymore.
You are the one watching, and the watcher is already still.

Breathe gently and let yourself sink a little deeper.
Below the busy mind, there is an emotional layer.
This is where your feelings live, some old, some fresh, all real.
Perhaps you find traces of worry, tenderness, frustration, or quiet sadness.

Notice these feelings without trying to change or resolve them.
They are like weather patterns, real and temporary and always shifting.
Let them be present the way clouds are present, part of the sky but not the sky itself.
You are the sky, vast enough to hold any weather that arises.

Breathe again and feel yourself descending another layer deeper.
Below the emotions, there is something quieter, something that does not move.
It may be faint at first, like a sound heard from very far away.
But it is there, steady and patient, the stillness beneath everything.

Imagine yourself standing at the shore of a deep, calm ocean.
The surface has waves, whitecaps, spray, constant motion.
But you know that below the surface, the water becomes still.
And far below, in the deepest trenches, there is absolute, perfect peace.

Now imagine yourself diving gently beneath the surface.
The noise of the waves fades as you descend.
The light softens, the temperature evens, the movement slows.
With each breath, you sink a little deeper into quietude.

Down here, there are no stories about who you are or what you should be.
There are no deadlines, no obligations, no performances to maintain.
There is only presence, pure and undisturbed, ancient and new at the same time.
This is the peace that has always lived beneath your surface.

Feel it now as a quality of stillness in the center of your body.
It may be in your chest, behind your heart, or deep in your belly.
It is warm and heavy and infinitely patient.
It has been waiting your entire life for you to arrive.

Now imagine yourself in a forest so old that even the trees have stopped hurrying.
The air is cool and still, filtered through a thousand green leaves.
No wind reaches the forest floor, only a soft, diffused light.
The silence here is not empty, it is full of a deep, living presence.

You sit at the base of the oldest tree and lean your back against its bark.
Feel the rough warmth of the wood behind you, solid and ancient.
This tree has stood in peace through storms, through seasons, through centuries.
It knows something about stillness that your busy mind has forgotten.

Let the peace of this forest seep into your skin like warmth from a fire.
Feel it in your bones, in your blood, in the quiet spaces between your cells.
This is not a peace you have created, it is a peace you have uncovered.
It was here before you arrived and it will remain after you leave.

Now imagine yourself as a great mountain, rooted deep in the earth.
Your base is broad and stable, your peak is high and clear.
Weather passes across your surface, rain, snow, wind, sun, clouds.
But your core remains unchanged, unmoved, perfectly at rest.

You are that mountain, and the weather is your thoughts and feelings.
They come and go, sometimes fierce, sometimes gentle, never permanent.
Your core does not react to the weather, it simply is.
This is the peace that cannot be disturbed because it does not depend on conditions.

Rest in this mountain stillness for a few quiet breaths.
Feel the solidity beneath the surface movement of your life.
Feel the silence beneath the noise, the calm beneath the turbulence.
This is who you are when everything else is stripped away.

The world will call you back to its surface soon enough.
The waves will resume, the weather will return, the thoughts will start again.
But now you know where the peace lives, and you know the way down.

The busy mind is not an obstacle, it is a doorway.
The emotions are not a barrier, they are a passage.
Everything on the surface is pointing you deeper, toward this stillness.

Take one more slow breath and feel the peace settle into every part of you.
It is not fragile, it cannot be broken or used up or taken from you.
It is the most durable thing about you, more lasting than any thought or feeling.

Carry this peace with you as you rise gently back toward the surface.
Let it inform the way you move, the way you speak, the way you listen.
The peace within you is not separate from the world, it is your gift to it.
And it is always, always, only one breath away.
