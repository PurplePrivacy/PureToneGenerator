Sit in a position that allows your spine to be straight and your body at ease.
Rest your hands in your lap and close your eyes gently.
Take a few deep breaths, letting each exhale carry you deeper into stillness.
Allow the muscles of your face to soften completely.

Bring your attention to the space between your eyebrows.
This is the ajna point, the seat of inner sight.
You do not need to strain or concentrate intensely.
Simply rest your awareness at this point, as lightly as a butterfly landing.

You may begin to notice subtle sensations here.
Perhaps a faint tingling, a warmth, or a slight pressure.
These sensations are natural signs of focused awareness gathering.
Simply notice whatever is present with patient attention.

Breathe gently and imagine your breath flowing to this point.
Each inhale carries awareness toward the space between your eyebrows.
Each exhale softens the tissue around it, creating openness.
The breath and the point begin to develop a quiet relationship.

As your attention steadies, you may notice a subtle light.
It may appear as a dim glow, a flicker, or a point of color.
The traditional color of this center is deep indigo, like a midnight sky.
If you see nothing at all, that is perfectly fine.

Imagine a small sphere of indigo light at the point between your eyebrows.
It is the size of a pearl, luminous and steady.
With each breath, it glows a little brighter.
Rest your full attention on this sphere of light.

Feel the forehead opening around this point of illumination.
The skin smooths, the muscles release, the bone itself seems to soften.
There is a sense of spaciousness developing behind the forehead.
Inside that space is a quality of seeing that goes beyond the eyes.

This inner vision does not look outward at the world of forms.
It looks inward, perceiving the patterns and movements of the mind itself.
From this vantage point, you can observe your thoughts without being pulled into them.
You become the one who watches rather than the one who is watched.

Feel the eyes relaxing more deeply behind closed lids.
Let them angle very slightly upward toward the ajna point.
This is not a strain but a gentle inclination, almost effortless.
As they settle, the mind grows quieter.

The indigo light begins to expand beyond the pearl-sized sphere.
It spreads across the forehead like the first glow of dawn on the horizon.
There is depth to this light, as if you could look into it forever.
You are gazing into the interior sky of your own awareness.

In this inner sky, thoughts appear like distant stars.
They are there, but they do not disturb the vastness.
You do not need to follow any of them or push them away.
The sky remains constant, unmoved by whatever appears within it.

Feel the stillness that gathers when the mind stops chasing its own contents.
You are more awake now than when your eyes were open.
The inner eye sees with a clarity that does not depend on light or form.
It sees the nature of things directly, before the mind adds its commentary.

Now let the indigo light extend deeper, into the center of the skull.
There is a space here, a quiet chamber of awareness.
It is a place of profound stillness where insight is born.
Rest here, in this interior sanctuary.

From this place, you can perceive the subtle web of connections in your life.
Questions you have been carrying may find their answers here.
Not through thinking but through a direct knowing that bypasses words.
Trust whatever arises from this silent place.

The witnessing awareness that lives here is not something you create.
It is something you uncover, something that has always been present.
It is the awareness behind your awareness, the seeing behind all seeing.
It is the most intimate and impersonal thing about you.

Now let the indigo light fill your entire head.
Your skull becomes a lantern glowing with this deep, penetrating blue.
You sit in your own radiance, quiet and composed.
The inner eye is fully open, resting in its natural state.

There is no effort needed to maintain this seeing.
It sustains itself the way the sky sustains itself.
This is your natural state when the noise of the mind grows still.

Feel the peace that comes from resting in witnessing awareness.
It is present whether your life is easy or difficult.
The witness is always at peace, because it is not involved in the drama.

Now let the indigo light gradually soften to a gentle glow.
Your forehead relaxes, and the eyes settle into ordinary rest.
But the sense of inner seeing does not disappear.
It recedes slightly, like a friend stepping into the next room.

Take a few deep breaths and feel your whole body present and alive.
Anytime you wish to return to this witnessing space, you can.
A gentle focus between the eyebrows is all it takes to open the door.
Carry this quiet seeing with you as you open your eyes and return.
