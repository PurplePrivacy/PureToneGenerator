Find a comfortable seated position and allow your spine to lengthen naturally.
Rest your hands over your heart, one on top of the other.
Feel the steady rhythm of your heartbeat beneath your palms.
Close your eyes and take a long, slow breath into the center of your chest.
Let the exhale be soft and unhurried, like a sigh of relief.

With each breath, direct your attention to the space behind your breastbone.
This is the center of your emotional life, the home of your deepest feelings.
Whatever is here, simply notice it without trying to change anything.
Your heart has its own wisdom, and it begins to speak when you listen.

Take another breath, and imagine the breath entering through the heart itself.
The inhale draws fresh, warm air directly into the heart space.
The exhale releases whatever the heart no longer needs to carry.
With each cycle, the heart softens just a little more.

Notice the muscles around your chest beginning to relax.
The intercostal muscles between your ribs loosen their grip.
Your sternum drops slightly, creating more room for the heart to rest.
The pectorals across the front of your chest widen and release.

Feel your shoulders rolling gently backward and down.
The collarbones spread like wings opening across the front of the body.
There is more space in the chest now, more room for the heart to breathe.
This physical opening mirrors an emotional one that is beginning to unfold.

Now call to mind a moment of pure, uncomplicated love.
It could be a person, an animal, a place, or even a fleeting moment.
Choose something that makes your heart smile without any sadness attached.
Feel the warmth that it generates in the center of your chest.

Let that warmth be the starting point for something larger.
Like a single candle flame, it begins to illuminate the space around it.
The warmth spreads outward from the center of your chest in all directions.
It moves into your arms and down to your fingertips.
It rises into your throat and softens the muscles of your face.

With each breath, the warmth expands a little further.
It reaches into your belly, warming you from the inside.
It moves into your back, melting tension between the shoulder blades.
Your entire body is gradually filled with the warmth that began in your heart.

Feel the heart now as if it were a flower slowly opening its petals.
Each petal represents a layer of protection you have built over the years.
In this safe, quiet moment, the petals open one by one.
The heart reveals itself, tender and radiant and unguarded.

There may be vulnerability in this opening, and that is natural.
An open heart feels everything more fully, both joy and sorrow.
But an open heart also has a capacity for connection that a closed heart cannot know.
The tenderness you feel is not weakness but a kind of courage.

Now begin to radiate the warmth outward beyond the boundaries of your body.
Imagine it flowing from your heart in a wave of gentle light.
It extends forward and behind, to your left and to your right.
You are surrounded on all sides by the warmth of your own open heart.

The warmth continues to spread, touching people you will never meet.
It reaches those who are suffering, those who are lonely, those who are afraid.
You do not need to know their names or their stories.
The warmth of an open heart reaches wherever it is needed.

Feel the immensity of your own capacity for love.
It is far larger than you usually allow yourself to experience.
The heart was built for this, for radiating warmth in all directions.
The more you radiate, the more the heart fills.

Now gently bring the focus back to your own chest.
Feel the heart resting in its open, soft state.
Place your hands more firmly over your heart as a gesture of care.
You can be open and also safe, tender and also strong.

Take a deep breath and feel your chest expand beneath your hands.
Each heartbeat is a pulse of warmth sent to every cell in your body.
You are loved from the inside out, by your own heart, in every moment.

Let your shoulders drop one final degree.
The space around your eyes softens, your brow smooths.
Your whole face takes on the expression of someone who has just been deeply held.
This is the face of an open heart, and it is beautiful.

Take a few more breaths in this state of gentle openness.
There is nothing to achieve and nowhere to arrive.
The heart knows this even when the mind forgets.
Trust the heart, it has been keeping you alive since before you could think.

When you are ready, lower your hands to your lap.
The warmth remains, steady and self-sustaining.
Open your eyes slowly and let the room come into focus.
Carry this openness with you gently into whatever comes next.
