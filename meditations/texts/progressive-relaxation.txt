Find a comfortable position lying on your back with your arms at your sides.
Let your eyes close gently and take a few slow, easy breaths.
We are going to move through your body one area at a time.
In each area you will tighten the muscles, hold briefly, and then release.
Pay close attention to the difference between tension and relaxation.
That contrast is where the deep letting go happens.

Bring your attention down to your feet.
Curl your toes tightly, squeezing all the muscles in both feet.
Hold that tension and notice how it feels, tight and compressed.
Now release completely and let your feet go soft and loose.
Feel the difference between the squeezing and this open softness.
Let your feet rest heavy and relaxed against the surface below you.

Now move your attention up to your calves and shins.
Point your toes away from you and feel your calves tighten.
Hold this tension and really feel it gathering in the muscle fibers.
Now release and let your lower legs go completely slack.
Feel them become heavy and soft like they are melting into the ground.
Your lower legs are now warm and loose and completely at ease.

Bring your awareness to your thighs, the front and the back.
Squeeze your thigh muscles tightly by pressing your legs together.
These are powerful muscles and they can hold a lot of tension.
Now let them go all at once and feel the release flood through.
Your thighs become soft and heavy and deeply relaxed.
Feel the weight of your legs sinking down with gravity.

Move your attention to your hips and your buttocks.
Squeeze the muscles of your seat and pelvis tightly together.
Notice where you store tension here in your daily life.
Now release completely and feel the muscles spread and soften.
Your hips open and your pelvis settles into the surface beneath you.
Everything from the waist down feels warm and loose and still.

Bring your attention now to your abdomen and lower back.
Tighten your stomach muscles as if bracing for something.
Notice the tension spreading around to your lower back as well.
Now let it all go and feel your belly become soft and round.
Your lower back releases and settles down toward the ground.
Let your breathing move freely through your relaxed belly.

Move up to your chest and upper back.
Take a deep breath in and hold it, feeling your chest expand.
Feel your upper back pulling together between the shoulder blades.
Now exhale fully and let your whole torso release and soften.
Feel your chest fall gently and your upper back spread open.
Your breathing becomes effortless and smooth in this relaxed space.

Bring your awareness down to your hands and forearms.
Make tight fists with both hands, squeezing your fingers into your palms.
Hold the fists tightly and notice the effort running up to your elbows.
Now open your hands and let them rest with fingers loosely curled.
Feel the release flow from your palms out through your fingertips.
Your hands and forearms are soft and warm and completely without effort.

Bring your awareness to your upper arms and shoulders.
Bend your elbows and tighten your biceps as if making a muscle.
At the same time, pull your shoulders up toward your ears.
This is where so many of us carry the stress of our days.
Now drop everything at once, arms falling and shoulders releasing down.
Feel the tension drain out of your shoulders like water flowing downhill.
Your arms are heavy and your shoulders are low and soft and open.

Move your attention to your neck.
Gently press the back of your head into the surface beneath you.
Feel the muscles along the back and sides of your neck engage.
Hold this gentle pressure and notice the work the neck muscles are doing.
Now release and let your head rest naturally without any pushing.
Feel your neck muscles soften and lengthen on all sides.
Your head feels balanced and light on a neck that is completely at ease.

Finally, bring your attention to your face.
Scrunch up your whole face, squeezing your eyes, clenching your jaw.
Wrinkle your forehead and nose and press your lips tightly together.
Now let it all go at once and feel your face become smooth.
Feel your forehead widen and your eyes rest softly in their sockets.
Let your jaw drop slightly open so your teeth are not touching.
Your lips are soft and barely touching, your cheeks relaxed and heavy.

Every muscle group has been tensed and released and is now at rest.
Your feet are soft, your legs are heavy, your hips are open.
Your belly is loose, your chest moves easily with each breath.
Your hands are warm, your arms are heavy, your shoulders are low.
Your neck is soft, your face is smooth, your jaw is loose.
Your entire body is deeply, thoroughly, completely relaxed.

Rest here in this full-body softness for as long as you like.
There is nothing more you need to do in this moment.
You are held by gravity and softened by your own intention.
This relaxation is always available to you whenever you choose it.
Carry this feeling of deep release with you as you gently return.
