Allow your body to settle into stillness.
Let the weight of your limbs be fully held by the surface beneath you.
There is nothing to fix in this moment, nothing to solve.

Close your eyes and notice your breath.
It arrives and departs without your permission.
Let it be exactly as it is, fast or slow, deep or shallow.

Begin by simply noticing what is present.
What does your body feel like right now?
What is the quality of your mind, busy or quiet, foggy or clear?
What emotional tone is in the background?

You do not need to change any of these things.
This is the first step of acceptance: simply seeing what is true.
Not what you wish were true, not what should be true.

Notice how the mind wants to adjust and improve.
It may want to make the breath deeper or the body more relaxed.
For now, let the mind observe that impulse without acting on it.

Say quietly to yourself: this is how it is right now.
Let those words settle in your body like a stone dropping into water.
There is no argument in those words, no struggle.

Feel what happens when you stop resisting what is present.
There may be a subtle softening somewhere in your body.
When we stop fighting the moment, the body often relaxes on its own.

Now bring your attention to your body as a whole.
This body, exactly as it is, right now.
It is the body you have been given, and it has carried you this far.

Practice saying yes to this body.
Not a forced enthusiasm, but a quiet, honest yes.
Yes to the parts you love and the parts you wish were different.

Your body has healed from illness and injury.
It has adapted to every circumstance you have asked of it.
It deserves your acceptance as a recognition of what it is.

Now bring your attention to your mind.
Notice its current state without trying to improve it.
Whatever it is doing right now is what it is doing.

Practice saying yes to this mind.
Yes to the racing thoughts, if they are present.
Yes to the inner critic, who is also just trying to help in its own way.
Your mind has worked tirelessly to keep you safe.

Now widen your awareness to your emotional landscape.
What feelings are present, even faintly?
Whatever is there, let it be there.

Practice saying yes to these feelings.
Feelings are not problems to be solved; they are weather to be witnessed.
They arise, they pass through, and they dissolve on their own schedule.

What if, just for this moment, you could let all effort go?
What if every feeling were allowed to be here, just as it is?
When you stop trying to manufacture certain emotions, something eases.

Now extend this acceptance to your life as it is right now.
Not as you planned it, not as you hoped it would be.
Can you say yes to this, even for one breath?

Acceptance and aspiration can live side by side.
You can accept where you are and still move toward where you want to be.
But the movement begins from acceptance, not from rejection of the present.

Think of the things in your life that you cannot change right now.
These things are already here; arguing with them only creates suffering.
Peace comes not from changing these things but from changing your relationship to them.

Say quietly: I accept what I cannot change.
Feel those words land in your body.
There may be resistance, and that is fine.
Acceptance is a practice, not a one-time achievement.

Now bring your attention to something you have been struggling against.
Hold it in your awareness without trying to fix it.
See if you can soften the edges of your resistance, even slightly.

Notice what happens in your body as you practice this.
Resistance takes an enormous amount of energy.
When you let go of even a fraction of it, energy returns to you.

Acceptance is the ground from which all genuine change grows.
A gardener does not curse the soil before planting seeds.
She accepts the soil as it is and works with what she has.

Feel the peace that lives underneath acceptance.
It is quiet and steady.
It is the peace of a river that flows around obstacles instead of crashing into them.

Take a slow, full breath and feel the wholeness of this moment.
Nothing is missing and nothing is broken.
You are here, alive, breathing, aware.

When you are ready, let your eyes open gently.
See the world as it is, without the filter of how it should be.
Carry this acceptance lightly; it will illuminate whatever comes next.
