Let your body find a position that feels safe and comfortable.
Allow your eyes to close and let go of anything you were holding.
Feel the natural rhythm of your breathing without trying to change it.
Each breath is like a small wave arriving and then receding.

Imagine yourself standing at the edge of a vast ocean.
Your bare feet are in the wet sand where the water meets land.
The sand is cool and firm and slightly soft beneath your toes.
You can feel the pull of the earth through the soles of your feet.

A wave rolls in and the water washes over your ankles.
It is cool and alive and gently moving around your skin.
Feel the soft fizz of foam dissolving against your calves.
The wave pulls back and the sand shifts slightly beneath you.
Another wave comes, and another, in an endless steady rhythm.
This rhythm has been going on since before humans existed.

Listen to the sound of the ocean in your imagination.
The deep rolling roar of water gathering and then breaking.
The hiss of white foam spreading across the flat sand.

Feel the salt air on your face and in your lungs.
The breeze coming off the water is cool and clean and wide.
Let this ocean air fill you with each breath you take.

Now begin to walk slowly into the water.
The waves meet your knees and then your thighs.
The water is cool at first and then your body adjusts.
You feel the push and pull of the current against your body.
Each wave lifts you slightly and then sets you back down.
You are in the zone where the ocean is most active and turbulent.

This churning surface water is like the busy surface of your mind.
Thoughts tumble over each other like waves breaking on the shore.
Worries and plans and memories crash and dissolve and reform.
It is noisy and chaotic and always moving here at the surface.
This is where most of us spend most of our time.

Now imagine you can breathe underwater, easily and naturally.
You begin to sink below the surface of the waves.
The noise of the breaking water fades as you descend.
Just a few feet down, the turbulence is already much less.
The water still moves but it moves in slow, rolling swells.
Light filters down in shifting columns of green and gold.

You continue to descend, slowly and without any effort.
The water around you becomes cooler and darker and more still.
You feel the pressure of the water holding you from all sides.
It is not uncomfortable but rather like a full-body embrace.

Go deeper now, down past the reach of surface light.
The water here is a deep, soft blue that fades toward indigo.
There is almost no movement at all at this depth.
The storms and waves on the surface are a distant memory here.
You are entering the vast quiet interior of the ocean.

This is like the deep stillness that lives inside you.
Beneath all the thinking and feeling and reacting and planning.
There is a place in you that the surface noise cannot reach.
It is always here, always quiet, always holding you.

Sink even deeper now into the profound stillness of the deep ocean.
The water is dark and cool and impossibly still around you.
You can feel the immense weight of the ocean above you.
But it does not press down on you so much as it shelters you.
All that water above is a buffer between you and the noise.

The ocean floor appears below you now, dimly visible and ancient.
Smooth sand stretches out in gentle ripples to the horizon.
Slow creatures move here in their own unhurried time.
Life here is quiet and deliberate and deeply at peace.

Rest here on the ocean floor for a moment and look up.
Far above you there is a faint shimmer of light.
The storms are still happening up there, the wind still blowing.
But from this depth, none of that matters or even registers.

You are the whole ocean, not just the waves on the surface.
You contain the crashing and the stillness equally.
All of it is you, and none of it is more you than any other part.

When life feels stormy and overwhelming and loud, remember this.
You can always sink down to your own quiet depths.
The surface may be wild but the deep is always there.
You do not escape the storm by going deep, you contain it.
You hold it all within your vastness without being damaged by any of it.

Now begin to rise slowly back toward the surface.
Feel the water warming and brightening as you ascend.
You break the surface and feel the air on your face.
The waves are still rolling in, just as they always have been.
But you carry the deep stillness with you now.

You stand again in the shallows with the water around your ankles.
You know what lies beneath the surface of things.
Breathe in the salt air one more time and carry this feeling with you.
You are the whole ocean, always and in every moment.
