Let your body settle and grow still.
Close your eyes and feel the quiet weight of your own form.
You are here, on the surface of a planet turning slowly through space.

Bring your attention to your breath.
Feel the air entering your nostrils, cool and alive.
Feel the air leaving, warm and soft.

The air you breathe has traveled across oceans and over mountains.
It has passed through forests and risen above deserts.
It is shared by every breathing being on this planet.

With your next inhale, imagine you are drawing in something more than air.
Feel yourself inhaling light from the stars, ancient and luminous.
Starlight travels for millions of years to reach this moment and your lungs.

Let this cosmic light fill your chest as you breathe in.
It is cool and radiant, carrying the energy of distant suns.
Your body receives it as naturally as it receives oxygen.

With your exhale, release your breath into the vast space around you.
Your exhale dissolves into infinite space without resistance.
There is room for everything you release; the universe is that large.

Inhale again and draw in the light of the Milky Way.
Billions of stars are shining right now, and their light is available to you.
Feel the collective radiance of the galaxy entering your body.

Exhale and let yourself expand outward toward those same stars.
Your breath carries your awareness beyond the edges of this room.
You are a point of consciousness in an infinite field.

With each breath, this exchange deepens.
Inhale: the cosmos pours into you.
Exhale: you pour yourself into the cosmos.
The boundary between inside and outside grows thinner with every cycle.

It is the same rhythm as the tides, pulled by the moon.
It is the same rhythm as the expansion of the universe itself.

Imagine that you can feel the earth turning beneath you.
You are a passenger on a living planet, spinning through space.

Now imagine pulling back and seeing the earth from a great distance.
A blue sphere hanging in the darkness, fragile and beautiful.
You are on its surface, breathing, alive, part of its story.

The sun, ninety-three million miles away, sends its energy to you.
Every photon that warms your skin began as a fusion reaction in the sun's core.
That energy traveled eight minutes through empty space to reach you.

Breathe in the sun's energy now.
Feel it as golden warmth entering through your skin and your lungs.
Its energy is patient, generous, and essentially infinite from your perspective.

Exhale and feel the warmth radiating back from your body.
You are a small, warm being in a vast, cool cosmos.
But your warmth matters; it is part of the total energy of the universe.

Now let your awareness expand even further.
Beyond the solar system, beyond the Milky Way.
Billions of galaxies, each containing billions of stars.

The scale is beyond comprehension, and that is perfectly fine.
You do not need to understand it; you only need to feel it.
Feel the vastness as a kind of freedom.

Inhale and draw in the dark matter that fills the spaces between stars.
It is invisible but it holds galaxies together.
You are breathing in the unseen scaffolding of the cosmos.

Feel how your body is made of cosmic materials.
The calcium in your bones was forged inside a dying star.
The iron in your blood came from a supernova explosion.
The hydrogen in your water is as old as the universe itself.

You are not separate from the cosmos; you are the cosmos looking at itself.
You are stardust that learned to breathe, to feel, to wonder.
Every atom in your body has a cosmic origin story.

Inhale the universe and feel it recognize itself inside you.
Exhale and let your awareness ripple outward like light from a new star.

Feel the silence of space surrounding you.
It is not empty; it is full of potential, full of energy.
In that silence, your breath is a small, beautiful sound.

Now slowly begin to contract your awareness back toward your body.
Feel the cosmic vastness settling into the familiar boundaries of your skin.

Feel your body lying here, warm and solid and breathing.
You are on the earth, under the sky, made of stars.

The cosmos is not somewhere out there; it is right here, inside you.
Every breath you take is a cosmic event.
Every heartbeat is powered by the same energy that lights the stars.

Take one more deep breath and feel the fullness of being alive.
When you are ready, open your eyes and see the world as it truly is.
Everything you look at is made of light and time and ancient fire.
