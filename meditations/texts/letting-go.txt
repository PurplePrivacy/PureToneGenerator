Allow your body to find a position of deep rest.
Let the muscles of your face soften, your jaw unclenching, your brow smoothing.
Feel the weight of your body being drawn downward by gravity, held by the earth.

Bring your attention to the simple act of breathing.
Notice how the inhale arrives on its own, filling you from the inside.
Notice how the exhale leaves without being pushed, a natural releasing.
Your breath already knows how to let go, it does it with every cycle.

Today you are invited to practice this same releasing with everything you carry.
Not forcing, not fighting, simply opening your hands and allowing things to pass.
Letting go is not losing, it is making room.

Begin with your body, the most immediate thing you hold.
Scan slowly from the top of your head to the tips of your toes.
Notice anywhere you find tension, tightness, or holding.
Perhaps your shoulders are lifted, your hands are clenched, your belly is braced.

Breathe into the first place of tightness you find.
Imagine your breath as warm water, softening whatever it touches.
On the exhale, let that tension stream out of you like sand through open fingers.
Feel the muscle release, feel the space that opens where the holding used to be.

Move to the next tight place and repeat this gentle softening.
There is no rush, each release is its own small gift.
With every area that softens, you become a little lighter.
Your body remembers how to be at ease, you are simply reminding it.

Now let your attention shift from your body to your mind.
Notice the thoughts that are circling, the familiar worries, the replaying conversations.
You do not need to stop them or judge them or engage with them.
Simply notice them the way you would notice clouds passing across a wide sky.

Imagine yourself sitting beside a gentle stream on a quiet autumn day.
The water is clear and slow, and the trees above are releasing their leaves.
Each thought that passes through your mind is one of those leaves.
Watch it land on the surface of the water and drift slowly downstream.

You do not chase the leaf, you do not pull it back.
You simply watch it float away, carried by the current.
Another thought arises, another leaf falls, another gentle release.
The stream carries everything away, and the water remains clear.

Now notice if there are worries you have been holding more tightly.
Concerns about the future, regrets about the past, stories about yourself.
These may feel heavier, more like stones than leaves.
But even stones can be set down beside the stream.

Imagine holding one of these heavier concerns in your hands.
Feel its weight, its familiar shape, the way your fingers know every edge.
You have carried this for so long that it almost feels like part of you.
But it is not you, it is something you picked up along the way.

When you are ready, place it gently on the ground beside the water.
Feel the relief in your hands, the lightness in your arms.
The concern does not disappear, but it is no longer your burden to hold.
The earth can carry what you cannot.

If there is another stone, hold it, feel it, and set it down.
You can release as many or as few as feels right today.
Each one you set down creates more space inside you.
Space for breath, for peace, for something new.

Now bring your attention to your heart and the emotional weight you carry there.
Old grief, lingering resentment, disappointments that never fully healed.
These live in the chest like weather, sometimes storms, sometimes heavy fog.
You do not need to push the weather away, only to open the windows.

Imagine your heart as a room with all its windows and doors sealed shut.
One by one, begin to open them, slowly, gently, just a crack.
Feel the fresh air enter, feel the staleness begin to move.
What has been trapped inside you is finally free to circulate and leave.

With each exhale, imagine old emotional weight lifting like mist in morning sun.
It rises, thins, and dissolves into the vast sky above you.
You are not losing anything precious, you are releasing what has finished its purpose.
Grief served you once, it taught you the depth of your love.
But you no longer need to live inside it.

Feel the growing spaciousness inside your chest as each layer lifts.
This is not emptiness, it is openness, room to breathe, room to receive.
The less you hold, the more space there is for life to move through you.

Imagine yourself now standing in an open field after releasing everything.
Your hands are empty, your shoulders are low, your face is turned toward the sky.
The wind moves through you as if you were made of air.
There is nothing to carry, nothing to defend, nothing to figure out.

This is what freedom feels like, not the absence of life but the willingness to let it flow.
You are the field, the sky, the wind, the open space.
Everything that comes will pass, and you remain.

Take a slow, deep breath and feel the fullness of this lightness.
Let it settle into your bones, this memory of what it feels like to release.
You can return to this place whenever the weight begins to build again.

The stream is always flowing, the leaves are always falling.
Your only task is to open your hands and let them go.
Carry this spacious emptiness with you as you gently return to your day.
