Allow your body to settle into a posture that feels both alert and at ease.
Feel the places where your body meets the surface beneath you.
Let your hands rest softly in your lap or on your knees.
Gently close your eyes or lower your gaze to a point on the floor.
Take one full breath and let it release completely.

Now bring your attention to the tip of your nose.
Notice the subtle sensation of air entering your nostrils.
There is a coolness as the breath flows in.
There is a warmth as the breath flows out.
This is your anchor, this small doorway of sensation.

You do not need to change your breathing in any way.
Simply observe the breath as it arrives and departs.
Each inhale brings a gentle coolness to the rim of your nostrils.
Each exhale leaves a soft warmth in that same small space.
Rest your full attention here, like a guard at a quiet gate.

The mind will wander, and that is perfectly natural.
A thought may arise like a bubble floating up from deep water.
You notice it without judgment and let it drift away.
Gently, with great kindness, you return to the breath at the nostrils.
This returning is not failure but the very heart of the practice.

Each time you come back, you strengthen the muscle of attention.
Each return is a small act of patience and self-compassion.
The breath is always here, always waiting for you.
It does not judge how long you were away.
It simply welcomes you back with another cool inhale.

Now imagine your mind as a glass of muddy water.
Each moment of steady attention allows a particle of dust to settle.
You are not forcing the water to clear.
You are simply holding the glass still.
The settling happens on its own when you stop stirring.

Layer by layer, the agitation begins to calm.
The surface of the mind grows smoother.
Thoughts still arise, but they are lighter now, less urgent.
They pass through like thin clouds crossing a wide sky.
Your attention rests more easily at the nostrils.

Notice how the breath has its own quiet rhythm.
There is a brief pause at the top of each inhale.
There is a moment of stillness at the bottom of each exhale.
These pauses are small windows of deep peace.
Allow yourself to rest in them, even for a heartbeat.

The clarity beneath the surface begins to reveal itself.
It was always there, hidden only by movement and noise.
You are not creating this stillness but uncovering it.
Your mind has a natural capacity for calm that runs very deep.
You are simply giving it the conditions to emerge.

If a strong emotion arises, treat it as you would a loud sound.
Acknowledge it, let it be, and return to the breath.
You do not need to solve anything right now.
You do not need to plan or remember or decide.
There is only this breath, this coolness, this warmth.

The glass of water grows clearer with each passing minute.
You can almost see through to the bottom now.
What lives there is a luminous, quiet awareness.
It has been present your entire life, waiting to be noticed.
This is your natural mind, undisturbed and bright.

Rest here in this growing stillness.
Let the breath breathe itself while you simply witness.
There is nowhere else you need to be.
There is nothing else you need to do.
This moment, this breath, is completely sufficient.

The mind may settle so deeply that even the breath grows faint.
This is a sign of deepening concentration, not a problem.
Trust the process and remain gently present.
If thoughts return, welcome them like old friends passing through.
Then turn your attention softly back to the nostrils.

Feel the quiet joy that comes from a settled mind.
This is not excitement but something deeper and more stable.
It is the contentment of simply being present.
It is the relief of laying down every burden, even briefly.
You carry this capacity for peace wherever you go.

Now allow your awareness to gently broaden.
Feel the whole body sitting here in stillness.
Notice the room around you, the sounds, the temperature.
Carry this calm with you as you prepare to open your eyes.
The stillness you found is yours, and it remains within you always.
