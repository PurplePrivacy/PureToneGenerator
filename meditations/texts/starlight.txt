You are lying on your back on a soft blanket spread across a gentle hillside.
The last traces of daylight have faded, and the sky above is opening into darkness.
The air is cool and still, carrying the faint scent of dry grass and distant wildflowers.
Let your body settle into the earth beneath you, feeling its steady support.
Your shoulders release, your spine softens, and the ground holds all your weight.

The first stars are appearing now, faint pinpoints of light in the deepening blue.
One by one they emerge, as though someone is gently lighting candles across the sky.
Each star you notice seems to invite the next, and soon the sky is alive with light.
You let your gaze soften, taking in the whole expanse without focusing on any single point.
The sky stretches in every direction, vast and unhurried, ancient and new.

The Milky Way begins to reveal itself as a great river of light across the heavens.
Billions of stars blur together into a soft, luminous band that arches overhead.
You are looking at the edge of your own galaxy, a hundred thousand light-years across.
This light has traveled unimaginable distances just to meet your eyes tonight.
Each photon arriving on your retina set out long before you were born.

Let that thought settle into you gently, without needing to fully comprehend it.
The light you see from the nearest star left its source over four years ago.
Some of these stars may no longer exist, yet their light still reaches you faithfully.
There is something tender in this, a gift sent across time with no expectation of return.
You are witnessing the past made visible, time itself turned into light.

Your body feels heavier now as you relax more deeply into the earth.
The ground beneath you is warm from the day, and it radiates comfort upward into your back.
You feel the slow rhythm of your breathing, easy and natural, like a tide.
Each inhale draws in the cool night air, and each exhale releases you further into rest.
The sky above is so vast that it seems to pull gently upward on your awareness.

You begin to feel the space between the stars as much as the stars themselves.
That darkness is not empty but full, holding galaxies and dust and possibility.
The universe is mostly space, and that space is alive with potential.
There is room in this sky for everything, and nothing is crowded or rushed.
You feel that same spaciousness opening inside your own chest.

A satellite drifts silently across the sky, a tiny point of steady light moving among the stars.
You watch it trace its arc, a human thing traveling through ancient space.
It disappears behind a cloud, and you return your gaze to the fixed stars above.
They are patient, these stars, holding their positions through millennia of turning sky.
Their constancy is a kind of companionship, quiet and reliable and vast.

You are made of the same elements that burn in those distant furnaces.
The carbon in your bones, the iron in your blood, the oxygen in your lungs were forged in stars.
You are not separate from what you see above you, but woven from the same fabric.
The calcium that builds your skeleton was released in a supernova billions of years ago.
You are the universe looking back at itself with wonder.

A shooting star streaks across the sky, a brief bright line that vanishes instantly.
It was a grain of cosmic dust meeting the atmosphere, transformed into a moment of light.
That flash of beauty required no effort, no intention, only the meeting of two things in motion.
You feel something lighten inside you as you witness it, as though you too can let go that easily.
The sky holds room for both permanence and impermanence, stars and shooting stars alike.

The night deepens and the stars grow brighter, more numerous than you thought possible.
There are stars behind stars, layers of light receding into incomprehensible depth.
You realize you are looking not just at space but at time itself.
The deeper you look, the further back in time you see, all the way to the beginning.
And here you are, alive and breathing on this small, warm, spinning world.

Your breath rises and falls like the gentlest possible wave on a calm sea.
You feel your heartbeat, steady and faithful, a small rhythm inside the great rhythm.
It has beaten without ceasing since before you drew your first breath.
In the scale of this sky, your life is brief, and that brevity makes it luminous.
Every moment you inhabit is as rare and unlikely as any star overhead.

The constellations turn slowly above you, tracing their ancient paths across the dome.
This same sky was watched by every human who ever lived, every eye that ever looked upward.
You are part of that unbroken chain of wonder stretching back to the first campfire.
The questions you ask under this sky are the oldest questions there are.
And the silence that answers is the same silence it has always been.

Let the starlight rest on your skin like the softest possible touch.
Let the immensity above you become a kind of comfort rather than a source of smallness.
You are small, and you are held, and you belong to all of this.
The stars do not judge or demand, they simply shine, and their light falls equally on all.
You breathe in the night, and the night breathes you.

Slowly, gently, you begin to feel your body once more on the warm ground.
The blanket beneath you, the cool air on your face, the rise and fall of your chest.
The stars remain above you, faithful and bright, whether or not you are watching.
You carry their light inside you now, woven into the very atoms of your being.
Whenever you look up, they are there, and whenever you look inward, they are there too.
