Allow your eyes to close gently and let your body settle.
Feel the weight of your body resting fully where you are.
Take a slow breath in, and let it flow out naturally.
With each exhale, let yourself sink a little deeper into stillness.

Now bring to mind the image of a mountain.
It can be any mountain you have seen or imagined.
Perhaps it rises from a green valley with forests at its base.
Perhaps its peak touches the clouds far above the treeline.
See this mountain in your mind with as much detail as you can.
Notice the shape of its ridgeline against the sky.
Notice the texture of rock and earth and snow.

This mountain is vast and solid and ancient.
It has been here for millions of years before this moment.
Its base is wide and deeply rooted in the earth below.
Its foundation reaches far down into the bedrock of the planet.
Nothing can move this mountain from where it sits.

Now imagine that you can become this mountain.
Your body sitting here is the mountain sitting on the earth.
Your legs and hips are the broad, stable base.
Your spine rises like the mountain's peak, tall and dignified.
Your head is the summit, lifted gently toward the sky.
Your shoulders slope down on either side like mountain ridges.
You are solid and still and grounded in this moment.

Feel the steadiness in your body right now.
Feel the weight of yourself pressing into the ground.
You are not going anywhere and you do not need to.
This is what it feels like to be completely rooted.

Now imagine the morning sun rising over your mountain body.
Warm golden light touches the peak first and then spreads downward.
It warms your chest and your arms and your hands.
The whole mountain is bathed in soft morning light.

As the day moves forward, clouds begin to gather.
They drift across the sky and cast moving shadows on your slopes.
Some clouds are light and wispy, barely there at all.
Others are thick and dark and heavy with rain.
The mountain does not move as the clouds pass over.
The mountain simply sits and lets the weather come.

Now the rain begins to fall on your mountain body.
You can feel it on your surface, cool and steady.
The rain runs down your slopes in small streams.
It gathers in pools and flows into rivers far below.
The mountain does not resist the rain or try to stop it.
The mountain receives the rain as part of what simply happens.

The storm grows stronger and the wind pushes hard against you.
Thunder rolls across the valley and echoes off your ridges.
Lightning flashes in the distance, bright and sudden.
Through all of this, the mountain does not move.
The mountain sits with the same quiet dignity it has always had.
The storm is real but it is also temporary.

And now the storm passes, as all storms do.
The sky begins to clear and the air smells fresh and clean.
Sunlight breaks through the clouds in long bright columns.
A rainbow arches across the valley below you.
The mountain did not do anything special to survive the storm.
It simply remained what it has always been.

Seasons change across the surface of your mountain body.
In spring, wildflowers open across the lower meadows.
In summer, the sun is warm and the days are long.
In autumn, the forests on your slopes turn gold and red.
In winter, snow covers everything in deep white silence.
Each season comes and each season goes.
The mountain is present for all of it and moved by none of it.

You can be like this mountain in your own life.
Thoughts and feelings are the weather that moves across your surface.
Some days are bright and clear and full of ease.
Some days are stormy and dark and full of difficulty.
You do not need to become the weather that passes through.
You can sit with whatever comes, solid and still and present.

Feel your own inner mountain right now in this moment.
Feel the stability that lives beneath all the changing weather.
No thought can move you from this place of stillness.
No emotion, however strong, can shake your foundation.
You have weathered every storm that has ever come to you.
You are still here, just as the mountain is still here.

Let yourself rest in this feeling of unshakable presence.
You do not need to fix anything or change anything right now.
You are the mountain, sitting with quiet dignity.
You are whole and solid and enough, exactly as you are.
Breathe in the stillness and let it fill every part of you.
Carry this mountain steadiness with you as you return to your day.
