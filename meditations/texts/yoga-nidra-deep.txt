Lie down on your back with your arms slightly away from your body.
Let your palms face upward and your fingers curl naturally.
Allow your feet to fall open to the sides.
Close your eyes and make any final adjustments to your position.
Once you are settled, resolve not to move for the remainder of the practice.

Take a deep breath in through the nose and release it slowly through the mouth.
Take another deep breath, filling the belly and chest completely.
Release it and feel your whole body sinking into the surface beneath you.
One more deep breath, drawing in stillness and calm.
Release it and let go of any remaining tension.

Now bring to mind your sankalpa, your heartfelt resolve.
This is a short, positive statement about your deepest intention.
Speak it silently to yourself three times with full conviction.
Plant it like a seed in the fertile soil of deep relaxation.
Trust that it will grow in its own time and in its own way.

You are now going to rotate your awareness through every part of your body.
Move your attention quickly and lightly, like a spotlight sweeping across a stage.
Do not try to move or tense any part, only feel it from the inside.
Begin with the right hand.

Bring your awareness to the right thumb.
The right index finger, the right middle finger.
The right ring finger, the right little finger.
The palm of the right hand, the back of the right hand.
The right wrist, the right forearm, the right elbow.

The right upper arm, the right shoulder.
The right armpit, the right side of the ribcage.
The right waist, the right hip.
The right thigh, the right knee.
The right shin, the right calf.

The right ankle, the top of the right foot.
The right heel, the sole of the right foot.
The right big toe, the second toe, the third toe.
The fourth toe, the fifth toe.
The entire right leg, heavy and relaxed.

Now move to the left hand.
The left thumb, the left index finger.
The left middle finger, the left ring finger, the left little finger.
The palm of the left hand, the back of the left hand.
The left wrist, the left forearm, the left elbow.

The left upper arm, the left shoulder.
The left armpit, the left side of the ribcage.
The left waist, the left hip.
The left thigh, the left knee.
The left shin, the left calf.

The left ankle, the top of the left foot.
The left heel, the sole of the left foot.
The left big toe, the second toe, the third toe.
The fourth toe, the fifth toe.
The entire left leg, heavy and relaxed.

Now bring your awareness to the back of the body.
The right shoulder blade, the left shoulder blade.
The upper back between the shoulder blades.
The middle of the back behind the heart.
The lower back, the right buttock, the left buttock.

The back of the right thigh, the back of the left thigh.
The back of the right knee, the back of the left knee.
The right calf, the left calf.
The right heel, the left heel.
The entire back of the body resting against the floor.

Now bring your awareness to the front of the body.
The top of the head, the forehead.
The right eyebrow, the left eyebrow, the space between the eyebrows.
The right eyelid, the left eyelid.
The right eye, the left eye.

The right ear, the left ear.
The right cheek, the left cheek.
The nose, the tip of the nose.
The upper lip, the lower lip, both lips together.
The chin, the jaw, the tongue resting in the mouth.

The throat, the right collarbone, the left collarbone.
The right side of the chest, the left side of the chest.
The center of the chest, the upper abdomen.
The navel, the lower abdomen.
The right groin, the left groin, the pelvis.

Now bring your awareness inside the body to the major organs.
Feel the brain resting inside the skull, soft and quiet.
Feel the eyes floating in their sockets.
Feel the tongue, heavy and still in the mouth.
Feel the throat, open and relaxed.

Feel the heart beating steadily in the center of the chest.
Feel the lungs expanding and contracting with each breath.
Feel the stomach in the upper left abdomen, warm and soft.
Feel the liver, large and heavy, on the right side.
Feel the kidneys, one on each side of the lower back.

Feel the intestines, coiled and warm in the belly.
Feel the bladder, low in the pelvis.
Feel the spine, a column of life running from skull to tailbone.
Feel the entire body as one living, breathing organism.
Every cell is relaxed, every organ is at peace.

Now become aware of the internal space of the body.
Imagine the space inside your skull, vast and dark and quiet.
Imagine the space inside your chest, where the heart and lungs float.
Imagine the space inside your abdomen, warm and expansive.
Imagine the space inside your pelvis, deep and grounded.

Feel the entire internal space of the body as one continuous cavern of awareness.
There is nothing to do in this space, nowhere to go.
You are simply resting inside yourself, vast and open.

Now bring your attention to the breath.
Begin counting your breaths backward from twenty-seven.
Inhale, twenty-seven, exhale, twenty-seven.
Inhale, twenty-six, exhale, twenty-six.
Continue counting each breath pair silently on your own.

If you lose count, simply begin again from twenty-seven.
There is no failure in losing count, only another opportunity to return.
Let the counting occupy just enough of your mind to keep you present.
The rest of your awareness can remain soft and spacious.
Continue until you reach one, or until the counting fades on its own.

Now you will experience pairs of opposite sensations.
First, feel your body becoming very heavy.
Every limb sinks deeper into the floor as if made of lead.
Your arms are heavy, your legs are heavy, your torso is impossibly heavy.
The entire body is so heavy it could sink through the floor.

Now feel the body becoming very light.
The heaviness dissolves and you feel weightless, almost floating.
Your arms lift slightly from the surface, your legs hover.
The entire body feels as light as a feather resting on still air.
You are light, buoyant, free of all weight.

Now feel the body becoming very hot.
A wave of warmth spreads across your skin like summer sun.
Your face is warm, your chest is warm, your belly radiates heat.
The entire body glows with a deep, penetrating warmth.
You are lying in bright sunshine on a hot afternoon.

Now feel the body becoming very cold.
The warmth withdraws and a coolness spreads across your skin.
Your fingers and toes tingle with a refreshing chill.
The air around you is crisp and cool like a mountain morning.
The entire body feels pleasantly cool and alert.

Now let both sensations dissolve into neutral awareness.
You are neither heavy nor light, neither hot nor cold.
You are simply present, resting in the space between opposites.

Now prepare for a visualization.
Imagine you are standing at the entrance to an ancient temple.
The stone walls are warm beneath your fingertips.
Sunlight filters through carved screens, casting patterns of light on the floor.
The air smells of sandalwood and dried flowers.

You step inside and feel the cool stone floor beneath your bare feet.
The temple is quiet, holding centuries of silence within its walls.
Tall pillars rise to a domed ceiling painted with stars.
In the center of the temple, a pool of clear water reflects the light above.
You walk slowly toward it, feeling peace with every step.

You sit beside the pool and gaze into the still water.
Your reflection looks back at you with calm, knowing eyes.
Beyond your reflection, you can see all the way to the bottom.
Smooth stones rest there, each one a shade of blue or green.
The water is so still it seems to hold time itself.

A single drop falls from somewhere above, creating perfect ripples.
The ripples expand in concentric circles, then fade to stillness again.
You feel those ripples moving through your own body, dissolving tension.
Each wave leaves you more relaxed, more transparent, more at peace.
You are the water and the stillness and the light.

Now let the temple dissolve gently from your awareness.
Return to the sensation of your body lying on the floor.
Feel the breath moving softly through your nose.
Remember your sankalpa, your heartfelt resolve.
Repeat it silently three times with the same conviction as before.

Begin to deepen your breath gradually.
Feel your fingers and toes, and invite small movements into them.
Rock your head gently from side to side.
Stretch your arms overhead and lengthen your whole body.
Take your time returning, there is no rush.

When you are ready, roll to your right side and rest there for a moment.
The practice of yoga nidra is now complete.
The deep rest you have experienced continues to work within you.
Carry this stillness with you as you rise and move through your day.
You are awake, you are refreshed, and you are whole.
