Let your body find a position of ease and comfort.
Feel the surface beneath you, solid and reliable, holding your full weight.
Allow your muscles to release their quiet gripping, one layer at a time.

Bring your attention to the rhythm of your breathing.
Notice the inhale as it opens space inside your chest.
Notice the exhale as it softens everything it touches on the way out.
You are safe here, in this moment, in this breath.

Place one hand gently over your heart if that feels right.
Feel the warmth of your palm through your clothing, through your skin.
This is you, offering yourself the simplest form of tenderness.

Forgiveness is not something you force or rush or perform.
It is a slow softening, like ice returning to water in spring.
It happens in its own time, and your only task is to be willing.

Begin with something small, a moment you wish had gone differently.
Perhaps a word you spoke too quickly or a kindness you forgot to offer.
See that moment clearly, without judgment, just as it was.
Now breathe warmth toward that version of yourself who did their best.
You were doing what you could with what you knew at the time.
Let that small weight lift gently from your shoulders.

Now bring to mind another moment, perhaps something that has lingered longer.
A choice you regret, a path you wish you had not taken.
Feel where that regret lives in your body, perhaps your stomach or your throat.
Breathe into that place with the same gentleness you would offer a child.
You do not need to approve of what happened to release the burden of carrying it.

Imagine that burden as a heavy stone you have been holding in both hands.
Feel its weight, its rough edges, the ache in your arms from holding it so long.
Now slowly, gently, set the stone down on the earth beside you.
Feel the relief in your hands, your arms, your shoulders as the weight transfers.
The earth can hold what you no longer need to carry.

Let your attention turn now toward someone who has caused you pain.
Start with someone whose hurt was small, a disappointment, a misunderstanding.
See their face in your mind with as much neutrality as you can find.
They too were carrying their own heavy stones when they stumbled into your life.
You do not need to forget what happened or call it acceptable.
Forgiveness is simply choosing to stop carrying their weight for them.
Imagine setting that stone down too, right beside the first one.

If you are willing, bring to mind someone whose hurt went deeper.
This is tender territory, and you may move as slowly as you need.
You do not have to complete this today or ever, only to begin.
See this person not as the source of your pain but as a whole human life.
They were once a child, confused and learning, just as you were.
Something in them was broken or afraid when they acted as they did.
This does not excuse them, but it may soften the grip of the story.

Imagine a door between you and this person, heavy and long closed.
You do not need to open it or walk through it.
Simply notice that the lock is on your side, and the key is in your hand.
Forgiveness is knowing you hold the key, whether or not you use it today.

Now turn your compassion back toward yourself one more time.
You have carried so much, forgiveness for others and judgment of yourself.
Place both hands over your heart and feel the steady beating there.
This heart has survived everything that has ever happened to you.
It deserves your kindness more than anyone else in the world.

Say silently to yourself, as if speaking to a dear friend in pain.
I forgive myself for being human, for learning slowly, for making mistakes.
I release myself from the prison of my own harsh judgment.
I am allowed to be imperfect and still worthy of love.

Feel the lightness that begins to arrive as these words settle into your body.
It may be faint at first, like the first warmth of dawn before the sun appears.
But it is real, and it will grow each time you return to this practice.

Imagine yourself standing in an open field after a long rain.
The air is clean, the ground is soft, the sky is wide and clear.
Everything that was heavy has been washed away, at least for now.
You are lighter than you were when you began.

Take a slow, full breath and feel this lightness fill your lungs.
Feel it spread through your ribs, your belly, your limbs.
You are free to set down what no longer serves you.
You are free to begin again, as many times as you need.

Carry this softness with you as you return to the world.
Let it remind you that forgiveness is not a single act but a practice.
Each time you choose release over resentment, you choose your own freedom.
And you are always, always allowed to choose freedom.
