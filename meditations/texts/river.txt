You are sitting on the grassy bank of a wide, flowing river.
The ground beneath you is soft and dry, and you settle into it comfortably.
The air is fresh and carries the faint mineral scent of clean water moving over stone.
Before you, the river stretches from left to right, wide and steady and unhurried.
Sunlight plays across its surface in shifting patterns of silver and gold.

The sound of the river reaches you as a continuous, layered murmur.
It is made of a thousand small sounds, water over rock, current against bank, ripple meeting ripple.
Together they form a single voice, deep and soothing and endlessly patient.
You let this sound fill your awareness, letting it become the background of everything.
The river has been speaking this way for longer than anyone can remember.

You watch the surface of the water and notice how it moves in different speeds.
Near the bank where you sit, the current is slow and gentle, almost still.
Further out, the main current flows with more purpose, carrying small leaves and twigs downstream.
A fallen leaf spins lazily in an eddy near the shore before the current takes it onward.
You watch it travel downstream and disappear around the far bend.

Another leaf falls from a branch above and lands softly on the water's surface.
It sits there for a moment, perfectly still, before the current gently claims it.
The leaf does not resist, it simply allows itself to be carried.
You notice how effortlessly it moves, how the water supports it completely.
There is something freeing in watching this surrender to the natural flow.

A thought arises in your mind, and you notice it the way you noticed the leaf.
You let it rest on the surface of your awareness for a moment, acknowledging it.
Then you let the current of your attention carry it gently downstream and away.
You do not chase it, you do not hold it, you simply let it go.
Another thought comes, and you do the same, watching it float past and fade.

The river carries everything equally, without preference or judgment.
A twig, a leaf, a feather, a blossom, each one is received and moved along.
The river does not cling to what passes through it and does not push anything away.
It simply flows, and in that flowing, everything finds its way downstream.
You feel your own mind beginning to adopt this same gentle quality of release.

A smooth, flat stone gleams under the clear water near your feet.
You can see every detail of it through the transparent current, its colors and texture and shape.
The water moves over it without disturbing it, polishing it slowly over years and centuries.
That stone is like the deepest, most settled part of you, steady beneath the flow.
Currents pass, seasons change, and that core remains, patient and unmoved.

You are the riverbank, stable and present, watching everything pass.
Sensations come and go like ripples on the surface, present for a moment, then dissolving.
Feelings move through like deeper currents, powerful but temporary, always flowing onward.
Memories drift past like boats on a wide waterway, visible and then gone.
You remain here, seated and still, the quiet witness to all this movement.

A kingfisher darts across the water in a flash of iridescent blue and orange.
It hovers for an instant above the surface, then plunges and rises with a silver fish.
The water closes behind it instantly, as though nothing happened.
The river receives every disturbance and smooths it away without effort.
You feel this same resilience in yourself, this capacity to absorb and return to stillness.

Further upstream, the water tumbles over a small cascade of rocks.
You can hear the brighter, livelier sound it makes there, a cheerful rushing.
Even when the river moves fast, it remains the river, the same water, the same essential nature.
Your own life moves sometimes quickly and sometimes slowly, but you are always you.
The speed of the current does not change the nature of the water.

A dragonfly hovers above the surface, its wings catching the light in transparent flashes.
It touches the water briefly and lifts away, leaving only the faintest circle expanding outward.
That circle grows wider and wider until it reaches you and dissolves into stillness.
Every action sends ripples, and every ripple eventually returns to calm.
You watch the surface settle and feel your own inner waters growing quiet.

The sun has shifted, and the light on the water has changed to a deeper gold.
Long shadows from the trees on the far bank stretch across the current.
The river keeps flowing, unchanged by shadow or light, carrying both with equal grace.
You notice how the water looks different in shade, darker and more mysterious.
And in the light, it sparkles and dances with a joy that asks for nothing.

You have been sitting here long enough that you feel the river's rhythm in your body.
Your breath moves like the current, steady and natural, without force or resistance.
Your thoughts have slowed to match the gentle pace of the water at the bank.
There is peace in this simple act of sitting and watching, of letting things flow.
The river teaches without words, and you learn by simply being present.

Take one more deep breath of this river air, cool and clean and alive.
Feel the ground beneath you, steady and supportive as the bank is to the river.
The sound of the water continues, faithful and constant, as you prepare to return.
You carry the river's teaching with you, the art of watching without grasping.
The flow continues, always moving, always fresh, and you are always welcome here at its shore.
