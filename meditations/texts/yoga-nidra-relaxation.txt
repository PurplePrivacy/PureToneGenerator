Let us now prepare for the practice of Yoga Nidra, the yogi's sleep. This is a time of deep relaxation and self-care, so please make yourself completely comfortable.

Position yourself lying flat on your back with arms and legs positioned slightly away from your body. Close your eyes.

Take a deep breath in through your nose and then let it go out through the mouth with a long soft sigh.

Repeat once more, noticing tensions dissolving. Allow your breath to flow naturally, becoming increasingly relaxed with each effortless breath.

Feel your body comfortable and motionless. In your mind's eye, visualize a small white dove. See the small white dove perching on a branch in the light of dawn.

Now is the time to make your resolve, your Sankalpa. Sankalpa is a brief positive statement that reflects your heart's highest aspirations.

Choose an affirmation such as I am love, I live in freedom, or I am already whole. Repeat your chosen statement three times with confidence and feeling.

We'll now take a mental tour through the body. As each part is named, let your attention move to that part and mentally repeat the name of that part to yourself.

Right foot, right calf, knee, thigh, hip, entire right leg. Left foot, left calf, left knee, thigh, hip, entire left leg.

Abdomen, navel, right chest, left chest, center of chest. Right shoulder, right arm, right palm, entire right arm. Left shoulder, left arm, left palm, entire left arm.

Throat, jaw, cheeks, eyes, forehead, entire face, top of head, back of head, neck. Right shoulder blade, left shoulder blade, spine, entire back.

Whole body head-to-toes. Toes-to-head. Entire body at once.

Now become aware of the breath, the spontaneous breath, as it flows in and out through the nose.

Count breaths backward from twenty-seven to one. Breathing in, twenty-seven. Breathing out, twenty-seven. Breathing in, twenty-six. Breathing out, twenty-six. Continue this pattern. If concentration lapses, begin again at twenty-seven.

Return to natural breathing and notice your body at ease.

Bring your awareness to the dark warm space in the front of your forehead, to the space of Chidakasha.

Visualize the white dove taking flight into the vast blue sky. Watch it soar gracefully across infinite space. Sense a peaceful, deep calm arising within you. Infinite calm, infinite peace.

Feel yourself united with the universe and all existence.

Recall your original intention and repeat it three times with confidence, knowing it manifests in your life.

Become aware of natural breath flowing in and out. Notice your body touching the surface beneath you. Visualize your surroundings and listen for sounds.

Make slow, gentle movements to awaken gradually. Roll to your right side and rest before slowly opening your eyes.

Here we complete this practice of Yoga Nidra.
