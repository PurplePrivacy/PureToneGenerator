Sit comfortably with your spine upright and your shoulders relaxed.
Let your left hand rest on your left knee with the palm facing up.
Bring your right hand up toward your face.
Fold your index and middle fingers gently toward your palm.
Your thumb will control the right nostril, your ring finger the left.

Close your eyes and take a few natural breaths through both nostrils.
Feel the air flowing freely through both sides of your nose.
Notice whether one side feels more open than the other right now.
The practice you are about to do will bring balance to both channels.

In the yogic tradition, the left nostril carries cooling, calming energy.
The right nostril carries warming, activating energy.
When both channels flow equally, the mind enters a state of deep equilibrium.
This is the purpose of nadi shodhana, the purification of the subtle channels.

Begin by gently closing your right nostril with your thumb.
Inhale slowly and smoothly through your left nostril.
Feel the cool air entering the left side of your nose.
Imagine the breath descending all the way to the base of your spine.

The breath arrives at the root, pauses, and gathers itself.
Now gently close your left nostril with your ring finger.
Release your thumb from the right nostril.
Exhale slowly and completely through the right nostril.

The exhale is unhurried, like a river flowing to the sea.
Let every last bit of air release before you begin the next breath.
There is a moment of natural stillness at the bottom of the exhale.
Rest there briefly, in the quiet space between breaths.

Now inhale through the right nostril, keeping the left closed.
The breath enters the right side, warm and activating.
Follow it down through the right channel of your body to the base of the spine.
The breath meets the root again, pauses, and prepares to change direction.

Close the right nostril with your thumb.
Release the left nostril and exhale slowly through the left side.
The breath exits cool and smooth, carrying away tension and fatigue.
This completes one full round, left in, right out, right in, left out.

You will continue this pattern at your own pace.
There is no need to rush or force the breath.
Let each inhale take four to six seconds.
Let each exhale take four to six seconds or longer.

As you continue, notice the breath's journey becoming more vivid.
The left channel may begin to feel like a cool stream running down your body.
The right channel may feel like a warm current flowing alongside it.
With each round, they become more balanced, more equal in strength.

Feel the air as it passes over the delicate tissues inside your nostrils.
The breath caresses thousands of nerve endings, sending signals of calm to the brain.
The parasympathetic nervous system responds with a gentle downshift.
Your heart rate slows, your muscles soften, your mind grows quiet.

Continue breathing, left in, right out, right in, left out.
With each round, the channels become a little cleaner, a little clearer.
Imagine the breath as a gentle wind sweeping through narrow corridors.
What remains is a passage of pure, unobstructed flow.

Notice the rhythm that is developing in your practice.
The inhale, the pause, the exhale, the pause, the switch.
It becomes almost automatic, like a pendulum swinging with perfect regularity.
The rhythm itself becomes a form of meditation.

Feel the balance beginning to establish itself in your body.
The cooling and the warming energies are equalizing.
Your body temperature feels perfectly even, neither too hot nor too cold.
There is a sensation of wholeness that comes from this balance.

The mind mirrors what is happening in the body.
As the channels purify, mental agitation begins to dissolve.
Thoughts slow down and become less compelling.
You are more fully present than you have been all day.

Continue for a few more rounds, savoring this growing equilibrium.
You may notice a subtle vibration in the center of your forehead.
This is a sign that the two channels are converging in balance.

Now release your right hand and let it rest on your knee.
Breathe naturally through both nostrils at the same time.
Notice how balanced and even the flow feels now.
Both channels are open, both sides of the body are at peace.

The breath flows in through two streams that merge into one.
It fills the lungs evenly, expanding the chest in perfect symmetry.
The exhale leaves through both nostrils in a single, unified current.
There is no division, no imbalance, only wholeness.

Rest here in this unified breathing for several cycles.
Feel the deep calm that has settled into your nervous system.
The muscles of your face are completely relaxed.
Your entire being feels washed clean by the breath.

The purification you have done today carries forward beyond this session.
The breath remembers what you have taught it today.
Take a few final breaths, deeper than the ones before.
Feel gratitude for this simple, powerful practice.
Open your eyes gently and let the room come into soft focus.
The balance you have cultivated remains with you, breath by breath.
